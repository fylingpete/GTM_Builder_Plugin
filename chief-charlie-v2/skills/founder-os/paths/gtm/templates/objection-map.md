# Objection Map — Top 10

> **Status:** empty_template — drafted in Step 2.2 from real lost-deal data
> **Team-required:** no (founder + first reps), reviewed weekly during 2.2 coaching
> **Source:** Hormozi's Kill Zombies + CLOSER + AAA Reframing + 1-to-10 Close

> **STRICT EVIDENCE RULE.** Every objection in this map must come from
> a REAL lost deal or stalled deal — not from "objections we imagine reps
> will hear." Skok's signal: lost-deal calls are gold. Listen to them
> first, then write this map.

## How to use this file

In 2.1 you closed ~5-10 deals yourself. You also lost or stalled some.
The lost ones tell you what's wrong with the playbook. This map is the
distilled version: the top 10 objections you actually heard, with a
reframe for each.

The map exists to (a) shorten new-rep ramp time and (b) prevent reps from
freezing when they hit a known objection. It is not exhaustive — if a
new objection shows up 3+ times, add it; demote one that's stopped happening.

Hormozi's frame: every objection is a buyer in pain trying to protect
themselves. Don't argue. Reframe. Ask questions about their question.
Address obstacles BEFORE you mention price (Kill Zombies).

## Rules (from the substance rubric)

- **objection_from_real_data (non-negotiable):** every entry traces to
  ≥1 lost or stalled deal call (with deal ID).
- **objection_top10_max:** more than 10 = noise. Reps can't memorize 30.
- **objection_has_reframe (not_argument):** reframe acknowledges the
  buyer's view first, then offers a different lens.
- **objection_has_evidence_proof:** each reframe has a piece of evidence
  the rep can drop in (case study, customer quote, data point).
- **objection_has_test_case:** at least one rep tested the reframe in
  a live call and reported back.

## General Reframe Frameworks

<!-- INSTRUCTION: When a rep hits an unfamiliar objection, fall back to one of these. -->

### CLOSER (Hormozi)

For full discovery-to-close arc:
- **Clarify** why they're here
- **Label** the problem
- **Overview** their past attempts
- **Sell** the vacation, not the plane
- **Explain** the solution
- **Reinforce** the decision

### Kill Zombies (Hormozi)

Address obstacles BEFORE you mention price. It's far easier to handle
concerns when stakes are lower. If buyer pushes back at price, you
already failed earlier — by leaving zombies (unaddressed concerns)
walking around.

### AAA Reframing (Hormozi)

Never answer the question directly. Ask questions about the question.
- Acknowledge what they said
- Ask what they really mean by it
- Address the underlying concern

### 1-to-10 Close (Hormozi)

When buyer is hesitant: "On a scale of 1 to 10, how ready are you?" Their
number reveals the real objection. Walk them from their number to 10.

### Listen 2× More Than Talk (Hormozi)

Buyers believe themselves, not reps. The longer the buyer talks, the
more they convince themselves. Resist the urge to fill silence.

### Never Ask "Do You Have Questions?" (Hormozi)

It hands the prospect the wheel and invites objections. Instead: keep
asking questions about their questions; reframe pushback as ethical
diagnosis.

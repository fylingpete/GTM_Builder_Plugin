# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> **Status:** empty_template — drafted at start of Step 2.1, locked when 5–10 repeat wins are closed via the same playbook
> **Team-required:** no (founder-driven, optional input from CS or first reps)
> **Source:** Skok's Phase 4 ICP framework + YC's "best first customers" stack-rank

> **STRICT TWO-HALVES RULE.** This document MUST contain both positive
> AND negative characteristics. The negative half is non-optional. Skok:
> *"Every 'no, this is not our ICP' decision in Phase 4 saves churn pain
> in Phase 7."* If the negative half is empty, the ICP is not done.

## How to use this file

The ICP is what makes every later team's job dramatically easier — and
what prevents wasted sales cycles, mis-fit churn, and scattered messaging.
You write it from real buyer-conversation evidence, not from your imagination
of "who would buy."

The positive half describes who you target. The negative half describes
who you actively reject. Both halves get equally specific. Vague ICPs
("B2B SaaS companies") aren't ICPs — they're TAM descriptions.

## Rules (from the substance rubric)

- **icp_has_two_halves (non-negotiable):** positive AND negative
  characteristics, both populated with ≥5 fields.
- **icp_has_evidence:** every characteristic traces to at least one
  buyer-conversation observation OR a closed/lost deal pattern.
- **icp_has_disqualifier_logic:** for each negative characteristic, you
  can state in one sentence why this disqualifies them.
- **icp_picked_one_market:** exactly ONE target market named — not "B2B
  SaaS" but e.g. "Series-B SaaS companies with sales-led motion in NA".
- **icp_has_urgency_filter:** the "hair-on-fire" moment that triggers
  buying is named explicitly.
- **icp_version_locked:** at end of 2.1, marked v1-locked when 5–10
  repeat wins are closed.

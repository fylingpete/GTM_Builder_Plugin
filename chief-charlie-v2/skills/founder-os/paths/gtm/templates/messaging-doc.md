# Core Messaging

> **Status:** empty_template — drafted at start of Step 2.1, locked when 5–10 repeat wins close via this message
> **Team-required:** no (founder-driven, validated against buyer responses)
> **Source:** Skok's Phase 4 messaging rules + Hormozi's SPCL/Proof-Promise-Plan + Theodore Levitt's quarter-inch-hole

> **STRICT FIVE-RULES TEST.** The core message MUST pass all five rules
> simultaneously: benefit-led, clear, simple, short, differentiated. Failing
> any one rule = not done. "Powered by AI", "automated insights",
> "next-generation platform" = generic. Same site as every competitor.

## How to use this file

The core message is what you say in the first 10 seconds — to a cold
prospect, on a landing page, in a cold email subject line, at the top of
a pitch deck. It is NOT your pitch deck. It is the headline that earns
attention long enough for the rest.

Skok's rule: write benefit-led messaging about the buyer's outcome —
generic positioning never closes deals. Theodore Levitt's anchor: *"People
don't want to buy a quarter-inch drill. They want a quarter-inch hole."*
Describe the hole, not the drill.

The message draws from buyer-conversation language — your buyers' actual
words about their actual pain. Founder-imagination messaging tests poorly
in market.

## Rules (from the substance rubric)

- **msg_benefit_led (non-negotiable):** business benefit, not product
  feature. The outcome the buyer pays for, not the mechanism.
- **msg_clear:** anyone in the buyer persona understands instantly. No
  re-reads required.
- **msg_simple:** no jargon, no internal vocabulary, no acronyms beyond
  buyer-standard.
- **msg_short:** fits on one slide or homepage hero — single sentence
  ideally, max 2.
- **msg_differentiated:** explains why this rather than the alternative.
  If the same sentence could appear on a competitor's site, it fails.
- **msg_from_buyer_words:** key phrases trace to buyer-conversation quotes,
  not founder language.
- **msg_use_case_specific:** speaks to a specific use case, not generic
  category language.

# Sales Playbook

> **Status:** empty_template — drafted in Step 2.1, expanded into full version in Step 2.2 with onboarding/coaching sections
> **Team-required:** no in 2.1 (founder-driven). Yes in 2.2 (rep-shareable artifact).
> **Source:** Skok's Phase 4-5 playbook discipline + Hormozi's CLOSER + YC's enterprise-funnel + Schatz's coaching black sheet

> **STRICT REPEATABILITY TEST.** A stranger must be able to read this
> document and execute it. Skok's exit criterion: *"the sales motion can
> be written down step by step (one target market, one use case, lead
> source, who to call, messages, engagement, pricing)."* If your playbook
> reads like memoir ("here's what I did with the deals I won"), it isn't
> a playbook.

## How to use this file

The playbook is the spine of every later GTM artifact. Phase 2.1 produces
the v1 spine — enough to do it yourself with conviction. Phase 2.2
expands it into something a non-founder can execute (objection map,
coaching rubric, onboarding plan).

When you write it, picture handing it to a competent rep on day 1 with no
shadowing. Where they have to guess, your playbook fails.

## Rules (from the substance rubric)

- **playbook_one_target_market (non-negotiable):** exactly one ICP +
  one use case + one channel + one motion described.
- **playbook_stage_by_stage:** each stage has trigger criteria, owner,
  artifact, expected duration, conversion rate.
- **playbook_demo_is_story:** demo is a story, not a feature tour. YC
  rule.
- **playbook_pricing_named:** price points are explicit, not "depends".
- **playbook_handoff_criteria:** explicit criteria for handing customer
  to CS team. Skok: vague hand-off = churn in 2.4.
- **playbook_executable_by_stranger:** in v1.5, a non-founder reads it
  and runs it. v1 may still need founder commentary.

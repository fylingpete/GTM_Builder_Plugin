## Pod-Pod-Pod Scaling — Matrix Partners (Stephanie Schatz)

The pod is the smallest unit of sales output that can be reliably modeled. Schatz's framing: once the first pod produces predictable bookings, the rest of the scaling decision becomes a question of "how many pods, how fast?" Everything else — individual rep performance, lead-supply mix, customer success ratios — is downstream from the pod math.

The discipline is treating pods as the atomic unit. Adding a half-pod (an AE without an SDR, an SDR without an AE) breaks the math and the manager's ability to coach.

### Pod definition — the contract

A pod has six locked attributes:

1. **Composition** — 1 SDR + 1 AE + ~0.25 CSM
2. **ICP** — same ICP as the validated motion, not a "let's try a new segment"
3. **Quota** — AE quota = 4-6× OTE per year, set at the start
4. **Lead-supply mix** — agreed percentage of inbound vs. outbound at pod start
5. **Coaching cadence** — weekly 1:1 with manager, monthly call-review session
6. **Reporting** — pod-level KPIs visible to the manager weekly

When all six are locked, the pod is comparable to other pods. When they vary, performance variance is uninterpretable.

### Pod-level KPIs

**Capacity Utilization** — (number of opps the AE actively works) ÷ (number of opps the AE could work at full capacity). <70% means lead supply is the constraint. >100% means leads are starving — slow them down to protect call quality.

**Pod Win Rate** — closed-won ÷ qualified-opps. Compare against the validated baseline from Phase 4. New pods should reach 80% of baseline within 3 months and 100% within 6 months.

**Pod Bookings** — AE quota attainment. The cleanest single signal of pod health.

**SDR-AE Conversion** — qualified-opps from SDR ÷ accepted by AE. <70% means the SDR is generating low-quality leads or the AE is too picky; investigate which.

### When to add the next pod

The signal: capacity utilization is consistently >70% AND pod win-rate is at ≥80% of baseline. Both conditions, both for at least 4 weeks. Adding a pod earlier wastes hiring spend; adding it later forfeits lead-supply that's hitting the ceiling.

When the signal fires: start hiring 4-6 months ahead of when the new pod needs to be productive. Hiring lead time is the actual rate-limiter at scale.

### Pod splitting at the manager threshold

When two pods are reporting to the same manager and the 8th rep is being added, the org splits: a second manager picks up one of the pods plus the new rep. The split is uncomfortable (managers and reps re-bond), but unavoidable — the 7-rep coaching limit is a hard constraint.

A common mistake: promoting the top AE of pod 1 to manage pod 2. The top AE's coaching skills are unproven; the AE position becomes vacant. Better: hire the new manager externally with explicit sales-management experience, leave top AE in their high-performing seat.

## Key Takeaway
Pods are atomic. Lock the six attributes (composition, ICP, quota, lead-supply, coaching, reporting) so variance between pods is interpretable. Add a pod when capacity utilization >70% AND pod win-rate ≥80% baseline. Split at the 7-rep manager threshold.

## When to Apply
- First pod is producing predictable bookings for ≥4 weeks
- Scaling plan calls for >1 pod in the next 12 months
- Capacity utilization metrics are being reviewed weekly
- Manager-of-managers transition is being planned
- Phase 10 org design is in progress

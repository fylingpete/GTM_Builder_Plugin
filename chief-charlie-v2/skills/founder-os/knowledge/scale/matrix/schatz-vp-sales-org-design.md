## VP Sales Org Design — Matrix Partners (Stephanie Schatz)

The first VP Sales has to design the sales org while running it — a job no playbook fully prepares anyone for. Schatz's reasoning: a sales org that worked at $1M ARR almost never works at $10M, and the redesign is what most VP Sales hires fail at.

The transition is from one generalist seller (the founder or the first AE) doing everything, to a specialized org where each role does one thing well. The transition fails when founders or VPs delay it — generalists who closed early deals will not close 10× more deals just by working harder.

### The three core seats — specialize early

**SDR (Sales Development Rep)** — outbound prospecting, top-of-funnel qualification. Generates discovery calls. KPIs: meetings booked, qualified opportunities created. ~$60-80K base + variable.

**AE (Account Executive)** — runs the demo-to-close cycle on qualified opportunities. KPIs: bookings, win rate, deal cycle. ~$80-130K base + variable, OTE typically $160-250K.

**CSM (Customer Success Manager)** — owns post-sale activation, expansion, retention. KPIs: NDR, logo retention, expansion ARR. ~$80-110K base.

The temptation: combine all three roles into one "full-cycle" AE. Schatz's data: full-cycle AEs cap out at ~60% of specialized-pod productivity once volume builds. Specialization wins above ~50 deals per quarter.

### Pod as the unit of replication

A pod is `1 SDR + 1 AE + .25 CSM`. Each pod produces predictable bookings once ramped. The math:

- SDR generates ~15-20 qualified opps per month
- AE converts ~25-40% of opps to closed-won
- CSM serves ~20-40 active customers depending on ACV

Scaling capacity = adding pods, not adding individuals. Adding an AE without an SDR creates a starved pod. Adding an SDR without an AE creates a backed-up pipeline. Pod integrity is the discipline.

### Management threshold — 7 reps maximum per manager

Schatz's rule: a sales manager can effectively coach up to 7 reps. Beyond 7, coaching dilutes and the manager becomes a forecaster, not a coach. The trigger for promoting a player-coach to full manager is the 8th rep.

The org evolves: 1 manager → 7 reps → 8th rep triggers a second manager → 14 reps → 3rd manager → 21+ reps triggers a "director" layer.

### Hire in cohorts of two

Hiring one rep at a time obscures whether failures are the rep, the playbook, or the manager. Schatz: hire reps in pairs. Two reps in the same cohort, same playbook, same manager. If both fail, the playbook or manager is broken. If one succeeds and one fails, the failure is the rep.

The first non-founder sales hires (Phase 5 in Skok's framework) are the canonical cohort-of-two test. Scaling continues with cohort-of-two as the default.

## Key Takeaway
Specialize early into SDR / AE / CSM. Pods (1 SDR + 1 AE + .25 CSM) are the unit of replication. Managers cap at 7 reps. Hire in cohorts of two so failures are diagnosable.

## When to Apply
- First-pod metrics are stabilizing — AE win rate, SDR opp-generation rate
- Founder is still running the weekly sales meeting personally
- The first sales manager is being hired or promoted
- Headcount plan for the next 12 months is being built
- Phase 10 org design is underway

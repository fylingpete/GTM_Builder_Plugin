## The Scorecard™ — EOS™ (Gino Wickman)

The Scorecard™ is the fourth EOS™ tool installed. It closes the data feedback loop around the weekly leadership rhythm. Most leadership teams steer their businesses by reading financial statements that arrive 2-6 weeks after the period they describe — by which time the past is unchangeable. The Scorecard fixes that lag by surfacing a small set of weekly numbers that move *before* the financial outcomes move, so the team can react while there is still time.

### The 5-to-15 rule (with bias toward 5)

A Scorecard carries 5-15 weekly numbers. The defensible upper limit is 15; the preferred target is closer to 5. The reasoning is the focus principle that drives the Rocks™ rule: when everything is measured, nothing is. Most first-draft Scorecards land between 8 and 12 and evolve downward over the first months.

### Activity-based, not P&L-based

Every Scorecard number must be a leading indicator — an activity that happens earlier in the chain than the financial outcome it predicts. This is the rule that separates a Scorecard from any other dashboard.

Examples of leading-indicator shapes:

- Weekly count of inbound qualified leads
- Weekly count of activities at each named sales-process stage
- Weekly cash-position snapshot at a fixed weekday
- Weekly accounts-receivable-aging marker
- Weekly customer-satisfaction signal captured proactively
- Weekly delivery / production throughput
- Weekly utilization / capacity for operations

A monthly revenue figure does NOT pass this test — it is the outcome itself, observed after the fact.

### One named owner per number

Each row has exactly one named owner from the leadership team. Not a department, not a committee, not "whoever runs the spreadsheet." The owner is the leader of the function that *drives* the number, not the person who keys the data in. A number with two owners is a number nobody is accountable for.

### The 13-week rolling grid format

Columns from left to right:
1. **WHO** — named owner (leftmost — structural elevation of ownership)
2. **MEASURABLE** — metric description
3. **GOAL** — weekly target
4. **Thirteen dated week-ending columns** with weekly actuals

The visible window is exactly one quarter of weeks — enough context to distinguish noise from signal. A single off-week is noise; three off-weeks in a row is a real trend.

### Red-flagging — turning grid into trigger

When a weekly actual misses its goal, the cell is visually marked (typically shaded red). The mark exists to pull the eye during the Scorecard Review segment of the L10™. Red cells drop to the Issues List for handling later in the meeting; unflagged cells flow past in silence.

### Report-only in the L10

The Scorecard Review is fixed at 5 minutes in the L10™ agenda. It is **report-only** — no discussion, no problem-solving, no debate about why a number missed. The instinct to launch into fixing is strong and must be suppressed; otherwise the L10 blows past 90 minutes and the truly important issues at the bottom of the list never get reached. Misses drop to IDS™ later.

### The desert-island framing

The exercise that strips a Scorecard down to its essentials: imagine the leadership team is cut off from the business and must run the company from a single sheet of paper containing only a handful of weekly numbers. Which numbers MUST be on that sheet? Almost every metric the team currently tracks fails the test.

### Goals tied to the 1-Year Plan

Each number has an explicit weekly goal — the threshold that distinguishes "on track" from "off track." In Phase 2+ (after the V/TO™ exists), each weekly goal derives from the 1-Year Plan section of the V/TO. That linkage ensures hitting every weekly goal delivers the annual plan by construction.

### Evolution — first draft is ~85% right

The first version of a Scorecard is not the final version. Most teams take 1-3 months of weekly use before settling on a stable list. Ship a credible first draft on day one and iterate weekly.

## Key Takeaway
5-15 weekly activity-based leading indicators. One named owner per row. WHO as leftmost column (ownership elevated, not buried). Red-flag misses. Report-only in the L10; misses drop to IDS™. Iterate the list over the first 1-3 months.

## When to Apply
- Step 3.2 after Accountability Chart™, Rocks™, and Meeting Pulse™ are in place
- Every week as item #2 of the L10™ agenda (5 minutes, report-only)
- Quarterly Planning Session: recalibrate goals if the underlying business has shifted
- Phase 2 transition (V/TO™ built): realign Scorecard goals to the 1-Year Plan

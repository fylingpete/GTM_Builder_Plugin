## The Meeting Pulse™ — EOS™ (Gino Wickman)

The Meeting Pulse™ is the cardiac rhythm of EOS™. Not a single meeting type — a system of three meeting types at three different tempos, each with its own purpose, length, agenda, and discipline. They are layered, and removing any one causes the other two to deform.

### The three tempos

| Meeting | Tempo | Length | Audience | Setting |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Weekly Level 10™ Meeting (L10™) | Every week, same day, same time | 90 min | Leadership team only | On-site fine |
| Quarterly Planning Session | Every 90 days | Full day (~8h) | Leadership team only | Off-site |
| Annual Planning Session | Once per year | Two full days | Leadership team only | Off-site, ideally requires travel |

### Weekly L10 — drift correction

The L10™ is the 7-day cycle. It exists to keep the leadership team focused on the small handful of things that matter week to week: the Scorecard™, the Rocks™, customer/employee signals, the To-Do list, the Issues List. Every owner reports on their numbers and Rocks in front of peers; any miss drops to IDS™ inside the same meeting.

Ground rules: same day, same start time, every week. 90 minutes with a hard stop. Same agenda every time. Held even when people are absent. 60 of the 90 minutes allocated to IDS — that's where the leverage lives.

### Quarterly Planning Session — priority reset

The 90-day cycle. Full day, off-site, leadership team only. Agenda:

1. **Segue** — best news (business + personal), what's working/isn't, expectations for the day
2. **Review previous quarter** — Scorecard numbers + Rocks scored binary; target ≥80% Rock completion
3. **Review the V/TO™** — refresh memory, resolve alignment loss
4. **Establish next quarter's Rocks** — run the 8-step Rock-setting process
5. **Tackle key issues** — IDS on biggest issues in priority order
6. **Next steps + communication** plan
7. **Conclude** — feedback round, 1-10 rating

Pre-work: every attendee arrives with their own list of issues + candidate priorities. V/TO is current as of the day before.

### Annual Planning Session — strategic refresh

The 365-day cycle. Two full days off-site, ideally travel involved so returning to the office is not tempting. Leadership team only.

**Day One** — backward review and strategic refresh: walk accomplishments + lessons from the previous year, score prior year's goals and prior quarter's Rocks, run open-room team-health exercise (each leader receives spoken feedback, commits to one change for next year), run SWOT to populate Day Two Issues List, walk the V/TO from Core Values down through 1-Year Plan, throw out last year's 3-Year Picture™ and build a new one.

**Day Two** — forward execution. Mirrors the quarterly agenda: set Q1 Rocks, IDS on key issues, agree next steps, conclude. The annual also serves as that quarter's quarterly session.

Team dinner at end of Day One — part of the team-health investment, not optional decoration.

### Why each tempo matters in its own right

- **Weekly = drift correction.** Without it, small issues compound into large ones and quarterly sessions become firefights.
- **Quarterly = priority reset.** Without it, the team has no forum for stepping back; the L10 turns into pure operations.
- **Annual = strategic refresh.** Without it, quarterly priorities slowly drift away from coherent direction.

The most common implementation failure is installing the L10 enthusiastically, declaring victory, and never getting around to the quarterly. Six months later the team is running disciplined weekly meetings on stale priorities.

### Pre-scheduling matters — the buildup effect

A two-day off-site that has been on the calendar since January generates a year's worth of accumulated thinking that discharges in those 40 hours. A two-day off-site scheduled in November for December produces nothing of the kind. Calling people into a planning day with a week's notice kills the mechanism — there is no time for preparation energy to accumulate.

## Key Takeaway
Three tempos, all required, none substitutable. Weekly L10™ corrects drift; quarterly resets priorities; annual refreshes the strategic picture. Pre-schedule the quarterly + annual calendar a year in advance to generate the buildup effect.

## When to Apply
- Step 3.2 onboarding — establish all three calendars before any other Traction tool
- Every quarterly review — confirm next quarter's date is still locked
- Every annual review — set the four quarterlies + next annual before the team leaves the room

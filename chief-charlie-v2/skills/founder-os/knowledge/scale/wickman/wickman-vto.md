## The Vision/Traction Organizer™ (V/TO) — EOS™ (Gino Wickman)

The V/TO™ — Vision/Traction Organizer™ — is the heart of EOS™'s Vision Component. It is a compressed two-page artifact that forces the leadership team to reach full agreement on eight questions about who the company is, where it is going, and how it will get there over the next twelve months and ninety days. The two-page constraint is the point: it makes vagueness impossible to hide. Whatever survives onto the V/TO is what leadership has actually committed to.

### Why the V/TO comes after the foundational tools, not before

The counter-intuitive choice in EOS™ is that the V/TO is installed fifth — after Accountability Chart™, Rocks™, Meeting Pulse™, and Scorecard™. The natural founder instinct is to start with vision; Wickman argues against it for practical reasons.

A vision session held by a team already living inside execution discipline produces grounded, debated planning. The team knows what the business can execute because they've been measured against weekly reality for months. A vision session held without that discipline produces aspirational fluff that nobody owns.

### The two-page structure

**Page 1 — Vision page (stable identity, slow-changing):**
- Left column: Core Values · Core Focus™ (Purpose/Cause/Passion + Niche) · 10-Year Target™ · Marketing Strategy (target market + three uniques + proven process + guarantee)
- Right column: 3-Year Picture™ (future date + revenue + profit + measurables + "looks like" bullets)

**Page 2 — Traction page (fast-changing execution):**
- Left: 1-Year Plan (date + revenue + profit + measurables + 3-7 annual goals)
- Middle: Quarterly Rocks (date + revenue + profit + measurables + 3-7 Rocks with single owner each)
- Right: Long-term Issues List (open numbered list of strategic items not urgent this quarter)

### Cadence — slow on left, fast on right

- Vision page changes slowly. Core Values and Core Focus may not change for years. 10-Year Target rolls forward only when within 3 years of it. 3-Year Picture rebuilt annually at the Annual Planning Session.
- Traction page refreshes frequently. Quarterly Rocks reset every 90 days at Quarterly Planning. 1-Year Plan resets annually. Long-term Issues List grows continuously.

### The eight questions in order

1. Core values (3-7, behaviorally testable)
2. Core focus (Purpose + Niche)
3. 10-Year Target (single, dated, measurable)
4. Marketing strategy (target market + 3 uniques + proven process + guarantee)
5. 3-Year Picture (vivid, datable)
6. 1-Year Plan (3-7 SMART goals)
7. Quarterly Rocks (3-7 priorities)
8. Current issues (long-term list)

Each layer is meant to derive from the layer above. A team that completes the questions in order produces a vertically coherent answer; a team that skips creates a cascade with gaps.

### Team off-site, not solo draft

The V/TO is built in a one- or two-day off-site with the entire leadership team. Solo-authored V/TOs are structurally invalid — the team's commitment is proportional to how much each leader contributed to writing it.

## Key Takeaway
The V/TO™ is two pages, eight questions, full leadership-team authorship. Vision page is stable identity (slow); Traction page is execution (fast). The cascade from 10-Year Target down to Quarterly Rocks must be derivable end-to-end.

## When to Apply
- Step 3.2 kickoff, in a 1-2 day leadership off-site after Accountability Chart, Rocks, Meeting Pulse, and Scorecard are in place
- Annual Planning Session: refresh 3-Year Picture and rebuild the 1-Year Plan
- Quarterly Planning Session: refresh the Rocks column and check the rest for drift
- After any fundamental business change (acquisition, major pivot, leadership change)

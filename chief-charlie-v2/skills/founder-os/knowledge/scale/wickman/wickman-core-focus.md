## Core Focus™ — EOS™ (Gino Wickman)

Core Focus™ is the answer to question 2 on the V/TO™ and one of the most under-appreciated disciplines in EOS™. On the page it occupies only a few lines, but those lines do load-bearing strategic work: they name the deeper reason the organization exists and the single thing it is exceptional at. Together they define the boundary inside which the company is allowed to play. Saying no to opportunities outside the boundary is the discipline — even attractive ones.

### The two sub-parts

#### Purpose / Cause / Passion — the WHY beyond money

The first sub-part: the reason the organization exists beyond making money. The team picks whichever of the three near-synonyms (Purpose, Cause, Passion) feels most natural. The required properties:

- **Stated in very few words.** A long paragraph is hedging. A real purpose collapses into a phrase.
- **Emotionally real.** Leadership can read it aloud without embarrassment.
- **Broad enough to outlast a specific industry.** Could carry into a different industry without losing meaning.
- **Survives a recession.** If still believed during a bad quarter, it's real. If only works when revenue is up and to the right, it's a celebration.

#### Niche — the ONE thing the company does exceptionally well

The second sub-part. Three words load-bearing:

- **Single.** Not a list, not a portfolio. ONE thing.
- **Exceptional.** Not "good at," not "competent at." Defensibly better than alternatives.
- **Today.** Descriptive of current reality, not aspirational. Evidence required — actual customers, actual outcomes, actual reputation.

### Together they form the "sweet spot"

The intersection of Purpose and Niche defines the sweet spot — the zone where the company concentrates energy. Inside, the company knows WHY it does the work (purpose) and IS exceptional at doing it (niche). Compounding advantage lives here.

### The discipline — staying inside the sweet spot

Once defined, Core Focus becomes the filter for every new opportunity. Decline anything outside the sweet spot, even profitable work. The case for taking off-spot work always sounds reasonable in the moment ("one-time engagement," "spare capacity," "only slightly outside our normal scope"). These opportunities are the primary failure mode for growing companies.

Every opportunity outside the sweet spot:
- Pulls leadership attention from work that compounds
- Forces building capabilities that don't strengthen the niche
- Creates internal complexity that slows everything else
- Erodes the clarity of the company's identity
- Tends to be lower-margin than expected (because the company is not yet exceptional at it)

A team that says yes to enough off-spot work eventually discovers it has become a different company than the one it intended to be — usually a worse one.

### Tests for whether Core Focus is real

**Purpose tests:**
- The recession test — still believe it if revenue dropped 50% next quarter?
- The industry-portability test — could carry into a different industry?
- The read-aloud test — can the leadership team read it aloud in an all-hands without anyone cringing?

**Niche tests:**
- The evidence test — visible current proof the company is exceptional (happy customers, repeat business, inbound referrals)?
- The single-statement test — one sentence without "and" or commas?
- The decline test — has the team turned down work in the last 12 months because it was off-niche?

### Common failure: confusing what you do with what you're exceptional at

Companies often offer 15 services but are exceptional at only one. The niche is that one — not the other 14. Ask of each candidate niche statement: "Is this what we DO, or what we are EXCEPTIONAL at?" Answers usually diverge sharply.

## Key Takeaway
Core Focus™ has two sub-parts — neither omittable, neither collapsible into the other. Purpose: emotionally real, recession-proof, industry-portable. Niche: single, exceptional, today (with evidence). The sweet spot is operational only if the team says no to off-spot work.

## When to Apply
- Step 3.2 V/TO™ session, as answer to question 2 of 8
- Annual Planning Session, as part of the Vision-page walkthrough
- Whenever the team is debating a major new opportunity — apply Core Focus before the financial analysis
- Whenever the team feels the company has lost its identity — usually off-spot work has accumulated

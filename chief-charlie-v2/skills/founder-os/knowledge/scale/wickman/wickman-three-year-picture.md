## The 3-Year Picture™ — EOS™ (Gino Wickman)

The 3-Year Picture™ is the answer to question 5 on the V/TO™. It is the only forward-looking element on the Vision page and occupies the entire right column on its own. Everything in the left column describes stable identity (Core Values, Core Focus™, 10-Year Target™, Marketing Strategy). The right column gives the leadership team a concrete, vivid snapshot of what the business will have become three years from now. The section is deliberately more emotional than the rest of the Vision page — its job is to give every leader something they can picture and want.

### Why three years specifically

- **Long enough to require real strategy.** Most teams can imagine 12 months out without changing much. Three years requires thinking about what the company will have built, hired, and learned.
- **Short enough to be vivid.** Unlike the 10-Year Target (single long-range goal), the 3-Year Picture is meant to be picturable. Three years is close enough that the team can actually imagine the office, team, product, customers.
- **The middle link in the cascade.** 10-Year Target above; 1-Year Plan below. The 3-Year Picture is the bridge.

Refreshed annually at the Annual Planning Session — rolls forward one year at a time.

### Required fields

- **Future date** — specific year-end three years out, aligned with the planning calendar. Concrete, not "sometime in 2029."
- **Revenue target** — revenue in the 12 months leading up to the future date. Single number, chosen after honest debate rather than rote growth multiplication.
- **Profit target** — at that revenue level, stated consistently (margin, EBITDA, whichever the team tracks). Forces the team to think about how the company will be making money at scale.
- **Measurables** — 2-3 additional KPIs beyond revenue and profit: unit counts, headcount, locations, capacity, product/service mix.
- **"Looks like" bullets** — ~10-20 bullets describing what the business will feel like, look like, and operate like. Vivid enough to mentally see, concrete enough to guide real decisions.

### Good "looks like" bullets vs. vision-fluff

GOOD: specific enough that the team could check, three years from now, whether it came true. Cover people (team size + quality + specific roles), physical/operational footprint, operational maturity, client/customer mix, product/service mix, market position, culture/rhythm.

BAD: phrases like "world-class experience," "industry-leading quality," "amazing culture." These are vision-fluff and do not belong on the 3-Year Picture.

### Position in the cascade

The 3-Year Picture describes a version of the company on track to reach the 10-Year Target. The 1-Year Plan describes a year that puts the company on track to reach the 3-Year Picture. The Quarterly Rocks deliver the 1-Year Plan. When the team walks the cascade end-to-end and any level does not trace cleanly to the level above, one of the two is wrong.

This traceability is the main reason the 3-Year Picture has to be concrete. An abstract forward picture ("become a leader in our space") cannot generate a concrete 1-Year Plan, which cannot generate concrete Rocks. Fuzziness at the top cascades downward and produces execution drift.

### Cadence — annually, not quarterly

The 3-Year Picture lives on the slow-changing side of the V/TO™ but is the element on the Vision page that changes most often: once a year, at the Annual Planning Session.

At Annual:
- Evaluate which bullets came true, which didn't, which turned out wrong to chase
- Roll the horizon forward one year
- Update revenue, profit, measurables for the new horizon
- Rewrite the "looks like" bullets as a new honest description

What the team does NOT do is touch the 3-Year Picture quarterly. The Quarterly Planning Session refreshes the Traction page; the Vision page stays as set at the last annual session.

## Key Takeaway
Vivid + concrete snapshot of the company 3 years out. Required fields: future date, revenue, profit, measurables, "looks like" bullets. Refreshed annually only — not quarterly. Bridges the 10-Year Target and the 1-Year Plan; traces both upward and downward.

## When to Apply
- Step 3.2 V/TO™ session, as answer to question 5 of 8
- Annual Planning Session, to roll the horizon forward one year
- Whenever the 10-Year Target is rolled down (company within 3 years of it)
- Whenever the business changes fundamentally — acquisition, major pivot, leadership change

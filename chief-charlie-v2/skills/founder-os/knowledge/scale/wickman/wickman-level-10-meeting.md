## The Level 10 Meeting™ (L10™) — EOS™ (Gino Wickman)

The Level 10 Meeting™ (L10™) is the weekly heartbeat of an EOS™ implementation. 90 minutes, same day, same time every week, leadership team only. It is the forum where the team confronts its numbers, its priorities, and its issues face-to-face. Priorities and metrics that do not get reviewed weekly quietly stop being priorities and metrics.

### The 7-item agenda with fixed time allocations

| # | Agenda item | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Segue | 5 min |
| 2 | Scorecard™ review | 5 min |
| 3 | Rock™ review | 5 min |
| 4 | Customer / employee headlines | 5 min |
| 5 | To-Do list review | 5 min |
| 6 | IDS™ | 60 min |
| 7 | Conclude | 5 min |
| | **Total** | **90 min** |

The agenda is the same every week. The team does not redesign it, does not shuffle the order, does not adjust the time boxes. Locking it down removes the decision from the team's mental load.

**Item 1 — Segue.** Each person shares one piece of good news (business or personal). One sentence, no discussion. Marks the context switch from working *in* the business to working *on* it.

**Item 2 — Scorecard review.** Each owner says "on-track" or "off-track" for their numbers. **Report-only — no discussion.** Misses drop to the Issues List for IDS™ later.

**Item 3 — Rock review.** Each Rock owner reports "on-track" or "off-track." "On-track" = genuinely confident of completing by quarter-end. Off-track Rocks drop to Issues.

**Item 4 — Customer/employee headlines.** One-sentence updates. Good news gets brief acknowledgment; bad news drops to Issues.

**Item 5 — To-Do review.** Every action item from last week scored binary done/not-done. Target: ~90% completion. A To-Do sitting on the list >2 weeks is itself a signal.

**Item 6 — IDS™.** The 60-minute core. Top-three issues by importance worked one at a time. Pick by priority, not by position on the list.

**Item 7 — Conclude.** Three things: (1) meeting leader reads back the new To-Dos so every owner has heard their commitment; (2) team decides what to communicate to the rest of the org; (3) each attendee rates the meeting 1-10, target >8.

### Same day, same time, same agenda — non-negotiable

Once the L10 starts moving around, it decays out of existence within months. The consistency is what removes the meeting from the team's mental load and turns it into a fact of the week rather than a decision.

### Roles — meeting leader + scribe

- **Meeting leader.** Runs the agenda, watches the clock, enforces the time boxes, facilitates IDS.
- **Scribe.** Keeps the Rock Sheet, Scorecard, To-Do List, and Issues List visible and updated live.

These are working roles, not honorary ones. The team functions noticeably worse without either.

### Start on time, end on time

Late starts cascade and the segment sacrificed is always IDS — the most valuable one. An L10 that runs over its hard stop blows up everyone's afternoon and trains the team to treat the meeting as elastic. Within weeks an elastic L10 is running two hours producing the same output it used to produce in 90 minutes.

### Devices off

Laptops closed, phones face down. The leadership team gets 90 minutes of full presence per week with each other, and this is it.

## Key Takeaway
90 minutes weekly. Seven agenda items in fixed order with locked time allocations (5/5/5/5/5/60/5). Same day, same time. Scorecard and Rock reviews are report-only; discussion lives in IDS™. Start on time, end on time. Meeting leader + scribe assigned.

## When to Apply
- Step 3.2 immediately after Rocks™ and the Meeting Pulse™ calendar are set
- Every week thereafter on the locked day and time
- Even when key people are absent — the L10 does not reschedule

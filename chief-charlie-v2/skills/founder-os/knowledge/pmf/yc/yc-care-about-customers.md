# Caring About Your Customers (YC)

> Source: Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell, YC Startup School: "Secrets You Can Learn From Your Customers"

**Why this matters for PMF**: The Sean Ellis 40% threshold tracks how attached users are to your product. Attachment compounds when the founder personally cares — not through dashboards, not through outsourced surveys, but through real relationships with individual users. Brex, Twitch, Airbnb, and many others credit the inflection in their PMF curve to the moment founders started genuinely caring about each customer as a person.

## The mindset shift: founder care is upstream — Y Combinator

Surveys, NPS, and data science are downstream artifacts. They summarize what already happened. Founder care is upstream — it's what produces the conversations, the trust, and the small unglamorous insights that nobody else in the company will ever surface. You can hire a great PM later. You cannot hire someone to care about your customers for you in year one.

## Brex: founders who were the customers

The Brex founders were in a YC batch. Their first customers were literally the other founders sitting around them — non-American founders under 25 who couldn't get a credit card from a US bank. The alternative the market offered them was nothing.

- They knew the problem personally because they had it themselves
- They asked friends: "Do you have a credit card? No? Want to try ours?"
- Their first ten customers were people whose stories they already knew
- Caring more than the big banks meant caring more than "no" — a low bar that they cleared by being present

The lesson: when you can be your own first user, your care is automatic and your insight depth is unmatched.

## Twitch: five years to the right kind of caring

For five years, Justin.tv had a complicated, professionally distant relationship with its streamers — many of whom streamed pirated content. The breakthrough came when two co-founders started calling individual video-game streamers personally. Not mass emails. Not surveys. One streamer at a time.

- Streamer: "Can we stream in higher resolution?" Fix: change a number in a settings file.
- Streamer: "Can you help us make money?" Fix: $20 checks, handwritten, mailed each month.
- Insight that emerged: help streamers quit their day jobs, and they will produce content millions want to watch.

That insight only surfaced because the founders cared enough to make uncomfortable, low-status phone calls one by one.

## Airbnb: showing up in person

The Airbnb founders flew to hosts' apartments to take photos themselves. They lost money on every visit. Hosts reused the photos on competing sites. But one host invited them in for coffee and shared a notebook of ten years of hosting insights — a goldmine that no survey, no analytics tool, and no hired researcher would ever have produced. That data only existed because the founders showed up and were trustworthy enough to be invited inside.

## Concrete tactics

- Run a weekly 30-minute 1:1 with each of your first ten customers
- Onboard every new user manually for as long as physically possible
- Hand off support yourself, especially angry tickets — they are the cheapest market research you will ever do
- Collect phone numbers at signup so you can call when something is confusing
- Keep a customer notebook: their goals, their language, their frustrations, their wins
- Show up in person when geography allows — coffee, office visit, lunch

## The compounding effect

Caring → conversations → learning → better product → more users care back → more conversations. The loop is slow at first and exponential later. The companies that hit 40% Very Disappointed almost always have a founder who was uncomfortably hands-on with the first 50 customers.

## Anti-patterns

- Treating customers as numbers in a dashboard
- Outsourcing support before founders have personally handled the first thousand tickets
- Optimizing funnels before understanding why anyone shows up
- "I'll talk to users once we have product-market fit" — backwards; talking to users is how you get there
- Designing by survey instead of by relationship

You cannot fake caring. Customers know. The founders who win at PMF are the ones who make caring their unfair advantage.

# Matrix Growth Framework — 12 Steps Reference

Source: David Skok, Matrix Partners — "Zero to 100: The B2B Startup Roadmap"

## Overview

The framework describes 12 inflection points a B2B startup passes through on the way from zero to scale. \
The 12 steps group into 3 macro-phases:

| Macro-Phase | Steps | User Phase |
|---|---|---|
| Search for Product/Market Fit | 1-3 | MVP |
| Search for Repeatable & Scalable & Profitable Growth Model | 4-8 | Go-to-Market |
| Scaling the Business | 9-12 | Scale |

---

## Phase I — Search for Product/Market Fit (Steps 1-3)

### Step 1: Test your Hypothesis
- Identify a specific problem for a specific customer segment
- Validate that the pain is large enough that customers are willing to pay
- Test whether your solution actually solves the problem

### Step 2: Prove the Value Hypothesis
- Show that customers derive real, measurable value from the product
- Early customers actively use the product and wouldn't give it up
- Net Promoter Score or "very disappointed" metric as signal

### Step 3: Prove it can be Sold
- Show that the founder can sell the product
- Develop a sales process that appears repeatable
- Understand the customer's buying process

---

## Phase II — Search for Repeatable & Scalable & Profitable Growth Model (Steps 4-8)

### Step 4: Build a Repeatable Sales Motion
- Develop a sales process that is teachable and repeatable
- Focus on ONE market segment and ONE use case
- Sell to multiple ICP customers outside your network

### Step 5: Prove Non-Founders can Sell
- At least one ordinary sales rep sells successfully
- Rep productivity reaches >4x OTE (On-Target Earnings)
- Founder hands over the sales process

### Step 6: Make it Scalable
- Consistent bookings growth over multiple quarters
- Proven, scalable lead sources
- Recruiting function works — new reps are successfully onboarded

### Step 7: Ensure Customer Success
- Customers are consistently successful with the product
- Negative dollar churn (expansion revenue > churn)
- Customer success process is systematized

### Step 8: Make it Profitable
- Unit economics work: LTV:CAC > 3
- CAC payback period < 12-18 months
- Gross margins > 70%

---

## Phase III — Scaling the Business (Steps 9-12)

### Step 9: Hit the Gas
- Aggressive scaling of sales and marketing
- Switch from burn-avoidance to growth mode
- Significantly more hiring

### Step 10: Scale the Organization
- Build management layers
- Processes and systems for 10x growth
- Deliberately scale culture

### Step 11: Expand Internationally
- Enter new markets
- Build local teams
- Adapt go-to-market to local conditions

### Step 12: Add Product Lines
- Expand product portfolio
- Systematize cross-sell and upsell
- Platform strategy

---

## Core Principle

> The #1 mistake startups make is jumping to the next phase before completing the current one. \
> Each step has clear exit criteria. Only when these are met should the next step begin.

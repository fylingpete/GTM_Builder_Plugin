## Phase 5 — Prove Non-Founders Can Sell — Phase 5, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

In phase 5 the question is: "Can someone who isn't a founder, and isn't a pathfinder, sell this?" The answer determines whether the playbook from phase 4 is real or imaginary.

### The Staffing Pattern

Two **ordinary sales reps** (not pathfinders) are hired. Founders deliberately step back from doing deals as their primary job. Their new role:
- **Hiring** the right rep profile
- **Onboarding & training** them on the playbook
- **Managing** to a clear ramp curve
- **Coaching** through deals
- **Building sales tools** — battlecards, demo decks, ROI calculator, objection scripts

Two reps (not one) is the rule. With one rep, you can't tell if a problem is the rep or the playbook. With two, the comparison reveals whether the motion is broken or the rep is wrong.

### Exit Criteria

Phase 5 is complete when:
- At least one ordinary rep is selling effectively (ideally both)
- Productivity is **> 4× OTE** (annualized bookings produced > 4× their on-target earnings)
- Customers remain happy and are realizing the promised business benefits

Productivity below 4× OTE is a warning. It means the unit economics will not support adding more reps profitably; phase 8 (make it profitable) will fail later.

### The Common Mistake

Founders fail to hand selling over because the new reps are visibly less capable than the founders were. The founders close one more deal "because the prospect is critical" — and another, and another — and never truly let go. Six months later there are still no proven non-founder closes and the company has missed the chance to know whether the playbook is real.

It is fine for founders to keep doing **a few** strategic deals. It is not fine for that to be their top priority.

## Key Takeaway
Phase 5 is the founder hand-off — hire two reps, step back deliberately, and use the comparison to test whether the playbook is real.

## When to Apply
- Just hired the first ordinary sales reps after pathfinder discovery worked
- One rep is failing and you need to diagnose rep vs playbook
- Founders are still doing every deal six months after the first rep was hired

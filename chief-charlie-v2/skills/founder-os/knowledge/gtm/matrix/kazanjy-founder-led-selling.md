## Why Founder-Led Selling Is Non-Negotiable — Matrix Partners (Mark Kazanjy)

In the early phases of a B2B startup the founder is the sales team. This isn't a temporary hack — it's a deliberate process of building the "sales software" that lives nowhere else. The founder is the only person who has accumulated 24 months of customer interview context, the early terrible demos, and the punches in the face from prospects. That accumulated knowledge becomes the playbook that can later be installed in someone else's head.

The progression maps cleanly to the early phases:

1. **Do we know what problem we're solving?** Grind through customer conversations until the answer is yes.
2. **Does it actually work?** Grind through product iterations until customers get the promised value.
3. **Will somebody pay for it?** Grind through monetization conversations until a stranger at arm's length writes a check.

Each stage unlocks a different magnitude of value, and the unlocks are non-uniform. The biggest one comes when the founder can finally **abstract themselves out of the day-to-day selling** — because a single founder is bottlenecked by 40-80 hours per week, and SaaS scale comes from running multiple parallel sellers.

The implication is structural: if only the founder can sell the product, the company has a ceiling. The whole point of phases 4-5 is to extract the sales software from the founder's brain, document it, and prove that other people can run it. Then it gets cloned out: 5 sellers, then 20, then 100. That's how SaaS companies "take over the world."

Skipping this work and trying to "sprinkle some sales on it" by hiring a VP of Sales early — before the founder has personally proven the motion — is the canonical anti-pattern that produces the famous "you have to kill a couple of VPs of sales before you scale."

## Key Takeaway
The founder is the irreplaceable bake-the-sales-software person — the rest of scaling depends on them doing this work first.

## When to Apply
- Founder is uncomfortable with sales and tempted to hire it out
- About to hire a VP of Sales pre-PMF
- Trying to figure out who should be doing customer conversations in early-stage

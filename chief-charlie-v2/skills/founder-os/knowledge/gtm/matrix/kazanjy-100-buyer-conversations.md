## 100-150 Buyer Conversations in Two Months — Matrix Partners (Mark Kazanjy)

The single most useful tactic for finding Product/Market Fit isn't a framework — it's a quota. Have **100-150 real buyer conversations** in roughly two months, with strangers who don't owe you anything.

The math: 15-20 conversations per week. The first month produces 80-100 conversations and the founder starts to pattern-recognize. The second month is for refining the value proposition and messaging based on the patterns.

### How to get the volume

The hardest part is generating enough conversations to start pattern-recognizing. Three practical methods:

1. **Hire a search engine marketer**, give them a budget, and have them run paid acquisition for two months. If they're competent, they will deliver leads in the raw — strangers across a wide spectrum of company sizes and pain points.

2. **Outbound prospecting** — direct outreach via LinkedIn, email, or cold call. Slow and uncomfortable but produces conversations with exactly the personas you want to test.

3. **Existing networks** — only counts if the people are real ICP fits, not friends doing favors. Selling to your uncle or to accelerator batch mates doesn't test PMF.

The signal you're looking for: prospects in the middle of actual decision-making processes who have a real problem, real budget, and real timelines. You will get punched in the nose repeatedly. That's the point.

### What to learn from the volume

Three questions, not one:
- **Who pays?** Who is the economic buyer? What's their job, company size, and budget authority?
- **For what?** What pain are they paying to remove? What outcome are they paying for?
- **How much?** What does pricing tell you about market segment (SMB / mid-market / enterprise)?

Pricing answers dictate the go-to-market motion. SMB requires a self-serve or low-touch motion; enterprise requires named accounts and a long sales cycle. Without 100+ conversations, founders guess wrong on segment and waste 12+ months building for the wrong motion.

## Key Takeaway
100-150 stranger conversations in two months — at 15-20/week — is the customer-discovery quota that produces real pattern recognition.

## When to Apply
- Pre-PMF, trying to validate the problem and the pain
- Tempted to start building before talking to enough buyers
- Stuck guessing about market segment, pricing, or ICP

## Quotas and Sales Compensation Design — Matrix Partners (Stephanie Schatz)

### Top-Down + Bottoms-Up — Always Both

Boards love top-down planning: "We did $3M last year — triple-triple-double-double, so $9M, $27M, $54M, $108M." The math is simple. The ground reality often isn't.

Always **counter with a bottoms-up plan**. A simple spreadsheet:
- Rows: every quota-bearing rep (and SDR/CSM)
- Columns: months of the year
- Cells: each rep's expected productivity that month, accounting for ramp time

Add up the cells. If the team you can realistically hire produces $6.7M, the $9M top-down number requires hiring more than 20 reps — likely impossible given ramp time and recruiting velocity. Better to commit to $8M with stretch, hit it, and earn credibility.

### Compensating Early Reps — Activities, Not Bookings

In the first months you don't know your average selling price, deal size, or close rates. Don't pay commissions on bookings yet. Compensate on **activity milestones** that change month to month:

- **Month 1**: write monthly customer-research documents (10 customers, what they learned, what they recommend)
- **Month 2**: 15 customer conversations / week
- **Month 3**: 7 proposals sent + 20 conversations
- **Month 4**: 2 proposals accepted + 10 sent + 25 conversations

As the funnel data stabilizes, transition to bookings-based compensation.

This requires significant trust both ways — you're paying people without a clean dollar metric. Pick reps carefully.

### Quota Achievability — The Winning Spirit

Don't set quotas designed to be 80% achieved on average. Don't fire the bottom 20% every quarter. Instead:

**Hire top-notch reps. Set achievable quotas. Build a winning spirit.**

A salesperson at 95% feels bad. A salesperson at 130% feels great. The dollar amount in your bank account may be similar, but the energy on the team is night and day. To support this, **you may need to slightly lower OTE** (on-target earnings) so reps consistently hit and exceed quota. The first 25 reps at Xamarin were hitting 120-130% routinely with monthly spikes to 160-170% — and the OTE was perhaps 20K below market. They went home celebrating, the company hit its numbers, and the comp at the end of the year was the same.

### Why This Matters

Phase 6 is about scaling sales reliably. Reliability comes from rep retention, rep confidence, and a culture that attracts other A-players. None of those survive a quota system designed to fail 20% of the team every quarter.

## Key Takeaway
Plan top-down and bottoms-up; pay early reps on activities until ASP stabilizes; set achievable quotas with maybe-slightly-lower OTE to build a winning spirit.

## When to Apply
- Setting next year's sales plan and quotas
- Hiring the first sales reps and unsure how to compensate them
- Morale is low because most reps are below quota despite working hard

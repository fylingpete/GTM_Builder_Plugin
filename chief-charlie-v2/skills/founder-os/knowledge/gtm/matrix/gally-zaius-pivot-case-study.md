## Zaius — How a Pivot Found a Real Repeatable Motion — Matrix Partners (Mark Gally)

Zaius (a B2C marketing-cloud platform) is a textbook recovery from a phase-4 mistake. The company had:

- **Six-month enterprise sales cycles**
- **A handful of large customers** that closed because of warm intros and infrastructure refreshes — not because the motion was repeatable
- **Multiple customer segments** within "B2C" (e-commerce, hospitality, even data resellers)
- **No consistent use case** across those segments
- **3-4 months of cash** when Mark Gally took over as CEO

The team had stepped on the gas treating warm-intro deals as evidence of PMF. Classic phase-4-jumping-ahead.

### The Recovery Playbook

**Step 1: Cut cash burn.** Reduce staff to extend runway during rediscovery.

**Step 2: Reset PMF discovery from scratch.** Hire a great search engine marketer, give them a real budget, and run paid traffic for two months. Goal: 15-20 buyer conversations per week, 100-150 in two months. Talk to **strangers in the raw** — people who don't know you and don't owe you anything.

The CEO personally takes every call. "I sat in the seat. We had no salespeople in the company. I literally chased every single lead down."

**Step 3: Reduce friction radically.** Zaius launched a freemium version of an enterprise product — six-figure deals turned into a free tier. *Freemium itself didn't work*, but eliminating the price-objection at trial revealed who the real buyers were.

**Step 4: Find the trigger event.** Zaius found that B2C companies refresh their email service provider on a cycle, and during that refresh they rethink the *entire* marketing infrastructure. That's the buying trigger. Inside that trigger they discovered the underlying need: "the B2C marketer doesn't have data, and their CEO keeps asking 'what's next?'"

**Step 5: Pivot the segment.** Enterprise wasn't right. Free wasn't right. Mid-market was the sweet spot — companies large enough to have the data problem and pain, small enough to buy without 6-month committees.

### The Result

Zaius's quarterly bookings chart shows the canonical "consistently jumping bookings levels" pattern Skok looks for in repeatable, scalable growth. The plan bars (white) get hit and exceeded each quarter.

### What This Case Study Proves

The PMF and phase-4 frameworks aren't theoretical:
- Warm leads ≠ PMF (they prove people will talk to you, not that the motion is repeatable)
- "B2C" is too broad a segment; one consistent use case is required
- 100-150 stranger conversations at 15-20/week, funded by paid search, work in practice
- Friction reduction (freemium-as-experiment) reveals real buyers even when the freemium model itself doesn't survive
- Trigger events are findable when you do enough conversations

## Key Takeaway
Warm-intro deals are the trap; the recovery is cutting cash, running 100-150 stranger conversations, finding the trigger event, and pivoting segments — and Zaius is the proof it works.

## When to Apply
- Founders convinced they have PMF based on a handful of warm-intro deals
- Sales cycles are long and inconsistent across customer types
- The team is considering a pivot but unsure how to structure the rediscovery

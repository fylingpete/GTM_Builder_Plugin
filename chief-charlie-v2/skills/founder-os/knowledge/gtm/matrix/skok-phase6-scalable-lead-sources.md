## Finding Scalable Lead Sources — Phase 6, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

Each individual lead source has a saturation point. Paid search exhausts the volume of buyers searching for your category. Facebook ads exhaust the targetable audience. Outbound exhausts the addressable list at a given response rate. Plotted over time, every source produces an S-curve that flattens.

A scaling B2B startup therefore must **layer multiple sources** so that as one source approaches its ceiling, another is ramping up. The pattern looks like overlapping waves:

- Quarter 1-4: paid search dominates
- Quarter 4-8: paid social adds on top
- Quarter 8-12: content / SEO / events / outbound layer in

Total lead volume keeps rising even as each individual source hits its limit. The job of the demand-gen function is to be constantly experimenting with the next source to add before the current ones plateau.

### The First Marketing Hire

The right first marketing hire knows how to **generate leads** — often called Demand Gen. Forget branding, marcoms, and PR for now; those are secondary. Branding doesn't fill a pipeline.

Great marketing at this stage is execution. Ideas are cheap; the question is whether the candidate has shipped lead-generating campaigns and can produce the next set. The interview signal: ask for a track record of campaigns they personally ran with measurable lead output. Vague references to "strategy" or "brand work" are red flags.

### Implication for Sales Plans

The bookings plan is only as good as the lead plan. If the demand-gen team's plan shows a saturating curve while the sales hiring plan shows a linear ramp, reps will run out of leads — and PPR will fall. Sales hiring plans should be locked to lead supply forecasts, not the other way around.

## Key Takeaway
No single lead source scales forever — build a layered demand-gen function whose job is to add the next source before the current one peaks.

## When to Apply
- Paid search or social ad spend is producing diminishing returns
- Hiring the first marketing person and unsure of profile
- Building the next year's sales plan and lead-supply assumptions matter

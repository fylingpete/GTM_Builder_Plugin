## Time to Wow! — The Product Is Your Salesperson — Phase 4, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

Credit Gail Goodman of Constant Contact for the framing: every product has a **Wow moment** — the first time a buyer sees something that makes them excited to continue. There are two flavors:

- **Mini Wow** — a moment that motivates them to keep exploring
- **Full Wow** — the moment that motivates them to buy

The strategic question is **how fast can the buyer reach Wow?** Three levers shorten Time to Wow:
1. **Number of steps** — fewer steps to Wow means less friction
2. **Time per step** — minimize delay (e.g. emails, manual setup)
3. **Friction per step** — every form field, decision, or required collaboration with a colleague is friction

### The Salsify Case Study

Salsify's original trial flow had 8 steps: Sign-up → Wait for email → Open the app → Learn the UI → Locate & Export Content → Sign up with new Retailer → Import Content → Export to new Retailer → Wow.

Most prospects dropped off at "Wait for email" and "Locate & Export Content" (which required IT support and another department). The Wow was real, but unreachable.

The redesign:
- Replaced the email-confirmation step with instant access (single email field)
- Offered "Explore with sample data" so the buyer could play immediately (Mini Wow)
- Added guided UI navigation so the buyer doesn't have to learn the app cold
- Auto-scraped the customer's existing retailer pages (eliminated the import step entirely)
- Final Wow: see your own data, fix problems, export to Google Manufacturer Center

Final flow: Sign up → See and solve problems with their own data → Export to Google → Wow. Three steps. **3× higher conversion** to taking action.

### Get Inside the Buyer's Head

For each step, ask: *what is the buyer thinking? Where would friction or doubt creep in?* Map their journey alongside the vendor's funnel and look for points where the two diverge — that's where conversion drops.

## Key Takeaway
Shorter Time to Wow means higher conversion — eliminate steps, time delays, and friction even when each individually seems small.

## When to Apply
- Free trial or demo conversion is low
- Onboarding takes weeks because of integration or import requirements
- Considering a major product or trial redesign

## The 11-Phase B2B Startup Roadmap — Matrix Partners (David Skok)

Conventional wisdom says a startup goes from Product/Market Fit straight to Scaling. That sequence works for B2C. For B2B it doesn't — turning up sales and marketing spend after PMF produces inconsistent results and heavy cash burn. There is a **missing middle phase**: the search for a Repeatable, Scalable, and Profitable Growth Model.

The complete journey breaks into three macro-stages and 11 sub-phases:

**Search for Product/Market Fit**
1. **Test Hypothesis** — "What are we solving? How?"
2. **Prove the Value** — "Does it actually work?"
3. **Prove it can be sold** — "Will someone pay?"

**Search for Repeatable, Scalable & Profitable Growth Model**
4. **Find Repeatable Sales Motion** — "Will many people pay?"
5. **Prove Non-Founders Can Sell** — "Can non-founders sell this?"
6. **Make it Scalable** — "Does this scale?"
7. **Ensure Customer Success** — "Can we scale without churn?"
8. **Make it Profitable** — "Can we scale profitably?"

**Scaling the Business**
9. **Hit the Gas and Scale** — "Does it keep scaling under pressure?"
10. **Scale the org and its processes**
11. **Etc.** — international expansion, additional product lines

Each phase is gated by a specific question. Until that question can be answered with evidence, advancing to the next phase wastes cash and time. Every phase change isn't just a milestone — the entire business motion changes (priorities, hires, focus).

The #1 mistake is jumping ahead before completing the current phase. Common variants: building product before validating the hypothesis, hiring salespeople before founders prove they can sell, scaling sales before the motion is repeatable, or scaling sales while churn is unsolved.

## Key Takeaway
Every phase is gated by a specific question — answer it with evidence before advancing, or you will burn cash you cannot get back.

## When to Apply
- Founder is unsure which phase they are in or what to focus on next
- Founder is about to hire salespeople, spend on ads, or expand internationally
- Board or investors are pushing to "scale" but the foundation looks shaky

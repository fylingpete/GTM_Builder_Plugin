## Buyer-Centric Funnel Design — Phase 4, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

The fastest way to fix a broken funnel is to stop designing it from the vendor's point of view.

A vendor-centric funnel has the prospect arriving at the homepage, watching a "How It Works" video, and pressing "Buy Now" — neat steps from the vendor's perspective, none of which match how buyers actually behave. The result is a funnel where every step is asking the buyer to do something they aren't motivated to do.

The fix: **map the funnel to the buyer's journey** before mapping the vendor's steps.

### The Buyer's Journey

Every B2B buyer is in one of three stages:
- **Awareness** — discovering they have a problem
- **Consideration** — evaluating ways to solve it
- **Purchase** — choosing a vendor and buying

Roughly 80% of website visitors are in Awareness, 15% in Consideration, 5% in Purchase. **Sales reps tend to handle every lead as if they're in Purchase** ("I'm paid to sell, I better be selling") — which destroys 95% of relationships.

### Different Content for Different Stages

| Stage | What works | What kills it |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| Awareness | Create problem/pain awareness; discuss how others solve it; benefits-focused content; **don't mention your product**; **don't sell** | Pushing demos, pricing, sales calls |
| Consideration | Help in choosing a solution; explain how your product solves the pain; free trial or demo; customer stories; nurture with newsletters | Generic broadcasts, no differentiation |
| Purchase | Case studies with results; ROI data; references; proof points | Vague promises, weak proof |

### Triggers Reveal Stage

Practical triggers that move buyers between stages: a hard-drive crash drives them to backup software; a new project triggers dev tool research; an outage drives APM searches. Knowing the triggers for your category lets marketing show up at the moment of stage transition.

### The Bank Account Metaphor

Treat the buyer like a bank account: **make deposits before withdrawals**. Every helpful piece of content is a deposit; every sales request is a withdrawal. Try to withdraw before depositing and the transaction fails.

## Key Takeaway
80% of leads are in Awareness — design content and outreach around buyer-stage, not vendor-stage, or 95% of relationships die before they start.

## When to Apply
- Funnel conversion is low despite traffic
- Sales reps are aggressive with cold leads and burning relationships
- Marketing content all reads like product brochures

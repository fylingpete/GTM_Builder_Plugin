## Phase 4 — Find Repeatable Sales Motion — Phase 4, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

At the start of phase 4 the sales path is a forest with no trail. By the end of phase 4 it is a clear, well-understood path that can be written down and taught.

What founders are trying to learn through ~50+ real buyer conversations:
- **Target market** — which one to focus on first
- **Buyer in the org** — who has the pain and budget
- **Use case** — the specific problem and benefit
- **Messaging** — the words buyers use to describe the problem
- **Sales motion** — how the deal moves stage to stage
- **Top-of-funnel** — what fills the pipeline (LinkedIn, paid search, outbound)
- **Pricing**
- **Missing product features** that block deals
- **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** and the negative profile (who NOT to sell to)

**Founder-led selling is non-negotiable here.** The role is best filled by founders plus one or two **pathfinder/trailblazer sales reps** — not ordinary reps. Pathfinders create and evolve the playbook; ordinary reps execute one. Hiring an ordinary rep into phase 4 fails almost every time and burns 6-9 months.

Two early decisions compound everything that follows: **pick one target market** (HubSpot's Brian Halligan: "we argued for years and by not deciding, every team had to serve multiple personas") and **pick one use case** (Crossing the Chasm "whole product" reasoning — the features, integrations, and partnerships needed for each use case are different, so deciding cuts the feature list to a manageable level).

**Hair-on-fire urgency** is the gating filter. Without urgency the conversations are pleasant but the deals never close — long sales cycles and few wins.

**Exit criteria for phase 4**: multiple ICP-fit customers happy and getting promised benefits, with a sales motion that can be written down step by step (one target market, one use case, lead source, who to call, messages, engagement, pricing).

## Key Takeaway
The output of phase 4 is a written playbook a sales rep could execute — not just deals founders closed using their personal network.

## When to Apply
- Founders are closing deals but cannot articulate a repeatable process
- Considering hiring the first sales rep
- Tempted to scale before the ICP and use case are nailed

## Don't Sell — Help the Customer Buy — Matrix Partners (Stephanie Schatz)

The framing matters. Great B2B salespeople don't *sell* — they help the customer buy. The reframe shifts the rep's job from "convince this person to give me money" to "remove every roadblock that stops this customer from buying."

The single most important thing any company sells is the perception that **you care**. The customer must feel that at every interaction — email, chat, phone, in-person meeting. Every touch must bring value. The discipline question for the rep before any outreach: *what value am I bringing to the table when I call them?* Without an answer, don't make the call.

### Three Characteristics of Great Reps

1. **Authentic.** Be your best self, but don't fake what you're not. If you're not technical, bring a sales engineer. If you're introverted, don't perform extroverted energy. The moment a customer senses a facade, the connection is dead.

2. **Genuinely curious / asks real questions.** Don't tick off Salesforce qualifying questions. Ask questions where you actually want the answer. Often something a rep learns from a customer in the morning is applicable in another conversation that same afternoon.

3. **Helpful in two specific ways:**
   - The intellectual horsepower to talk intelligently about a technical product
   - Operational control of their own organization so they can make things happen for the customer (nothing kills trust faster than a rep who can't get answers from their own team)

### Why this matters at each phase

- **Phase 4 (find repeatable motion):** every rep interaction shapes how the buyer talks about you. Pushy reps poison word-of-mouth.
- **Phase 5 (non-founders sell):** new reps need this philosophy explicitly taught — they didn't earn it through founder-led discovery.
- **Phase 6+ (scale):** the philosophy must survive scale. Hiring and onboarding rituals exist to protect it from being diluted by the wrong rep profiles.

The implication for sales hiring: the airport-test ("would I tolerate a 3-hour layover with this person") is too low a bar. The right bar is **"would I go on a weekend skiing trip with them?"** Because in early-stage you'll be in late-night rooms with this person — and they'll shape your culture as much as a co-founder.

## Key Takeaway
Help the customer buy by removing roadblocks; make every interaction add value; hire reps who are authentic, genuinely curious, and operationally effective.

## When to Apply
- Designing the sales rep hiring scorecard
- Onboarding the first ordinary sales reps
- Customer satisfaction scores or reviews are mixed

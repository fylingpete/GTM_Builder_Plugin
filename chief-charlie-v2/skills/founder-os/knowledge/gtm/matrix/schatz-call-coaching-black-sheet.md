## Onboarding Reps via Black Sheet and Debriefs — Matrix Partners (Stephanie Schatz)

After hiring the first sales reps in phase 5, the founder's job is to extract the sales knowledge from their head and install it in the reps' heads. The fastest way isn't training documents — it's structured time **before and after** every customer call.

### Mock Calls First, Then Joint Calls

New reps are intimidated. They're working with founders who are deep product experts. Make the first practice runs informal: pizza, beer, mock calls in the office. Lower the threshold to make mistakes.

After mock calls, do as many **joint customer calls** as possible. The rep observes, then does parts, then leads with the founder backing up.

### The Black Sheet — No Call Without Preparation

Adopt a strict rule: **no call without a black sheet.** A "black sheet" is a Google Doc per account with:

- Account basics at the top (industry, size, key contacts)
- For each call:
  - Participants
  - **Objective** of the call
  - **Agenda** (sent to the customer beforehand)
  - Notes during the call
  - To-dos out of the call
  - Follow-up email drafted in the document (sometimes started while still on the call if you can multitask)

Attach the black sheet to the Salesforce account. When territories get reshuffled or a rep leaves, the next person inherits the entire history. This compounds dramatically once the team grows.

### The Pre-Call Strategy Session

For early reps, the founder spends time with them before each call:
- *Why* are you phrasing it that way?
- *Why* are you not mentioning competition?
- What pattern of objection are you expecting?

This is where the founder's pattern recognition transfers fastest. It feels expensive in time. It pays back 10x in rep ramp speed.

### The Three-Question Debrief

After every call, a short structured debrief:

1. **What did we do well?** *(Praise — new reps are nervous and need calibration on what "good" looks like.)*
2. **What didn't go well?** *(Create an atmosphere where it's OK to criticize the founder/CEO. Model it by criticizing yourself first: "I wish I hadn't said X.")*
3. **What can we blueprint?** *(Once or twice a week, something will go catastrophically wrong or amazingly well. Document it. Add it to the sales enablement playbook. Xamarin scaled from 0 to 100 sales reps using exactly this practice.)*

### Why This Beats Training Decks

Training decks are static. The black sheet + debrief practice produces a **living playbook** built from the actual deals you're winning and losing — and the playbook accumulates institutional knowledge that survives rep churn.

## Key Takeaway
Mock calls + joint calls + black sheets + three-question debriefs is the founder-to-rep knowledge-transfer system; everything else is a poor substitute.

## When to Apply
- New sales reps just joined and you need them ramping fast
- You can't articulate the playbook well enough to hand to someone else
- Reps are leaving and taking institutional knowledge with them

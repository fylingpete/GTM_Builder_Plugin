## Phase 7 — Ensure Customer Success — Phase 7, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

Customer success matters in every phase, but it gets its own gate at phase 7 because **churn frequently spikes when sales starts scaling**. The motion that worked with founder-led, ICP-perfect deals breaks when reps push the same offer to whoever will sign.

### Diagnostic: Why Churn Goes Up at Scale

Several causes, usually overlapping:

1. **ICP drift** — reps are closing customers the founders would never have sold to. Tighten the ICP definition (positive *and* negative characteristics), train reps on disqualification, and back the discipline with comp structure.

2. **Sales over-promising** — closes get easier when reps imply features that don't exist or commit to outcomes the product can't reliably deliver. Best fix: **adjust sales compensation to claw back commission on customers who churn within N months**. The incentive aligns; the over-promising stops.

3. **Product gaps that customer success was hiding** at small scale. When a hands-on CSM held two customers' hands through a missing feature, the product looked fine. With twenty customers, it doesn't. Fix the root product issues.

4. **Onboarding** — at small volumes the onboarding was bespoke. At higher volume it must become a repeatable process with milestones, success criteria, and a clear definition of "activated."

### Ideal Staffing

Customer Success becomes a real function in this phase, with a leader and dedicated CSMs. The CSM job is not support; it is to make sure customers reach the activation point and the recurring value moments that drive renewal.

### Common Mistake

Hitting the gas (phase 9) when churn is still too high. Net-new ARR keeps growing for a quarter or two, then the churn drag overwhelms new bookings and growth flattens. The fix gets harder with every additional bad-fit customer added.

### Exit Criteria

- Customers consistently successful and happy
- **Negative dollar churn** — net dollar retention > 100% (expansion exceeds churn)

## Key Takeaway
When churn spikes at scale, fix ICP discipline, sales incentives, product gaps, and onboarding before hiring more reps — adding reps amplifies the leak.

## When to Apply
- Logo or dollar churn rose after sales scaled
- Customer success team is overwhelmed by escalations
- Considering hitting the gas but unsure if the foundation is solid

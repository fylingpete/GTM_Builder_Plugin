## The Two Stories: Browsing Sweater vs Black-Tie Wedding — Phase 4, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

A simple thought experiment makes the buyer-stage point unforgettable.

### Story 1 — The Sweater

You're at the train station, half an hour early, picking up your partner. You wander into a clothing store to kill time. Within seconds a salesperson approaches: "Can I help you, sir?" You say "no thanks, just browsing." They hover nearby. You pick up a sweater. They pounce: "Let me tell you about that one — Italian wool, on sale this quarter at half price, looks like something you might like."

How do you feel about that salesperson?

When asked, audiences overwhelmingly **hate** that salesperson. The interaction is irritating because you're nowhere near buying. You're in pure Awareness/browse mode and you've been forced into a Purchase-stage interaction.

### Story 2 — The Black Tie

You have a black-tie wedding tonight. You don't own a black tie. You walk into Nordstrom's. You can't find the tie. You wander the men's section searching for help. **Not a salesperson in sight.**

How do you feel?

This time the audience equally **hates the absence** of a salesperson. You're in Purchase stage with high urgency, and the system isn't giving you what you need.

### The Lesson

Same buyer, same store category, opposite reactions — driven entirely by the buyer's stage in the journey. Awareness-stage buyers want to be left alone with helpful information. Purchase-stage buyers want responsive help.

The implication for B2B funnels: roughly 80% of website visitors are in Awareness, 15% in Consideration, 5% in Purchase. Sales reps treating every lead as if they're in Purchase repel 95% of relationships. Marketing not surfacing high-intent buyers fast to sales fails the 5% who are ready.

The fix is stage-appropriate content and engagement:
- **Awareness** → educational content, problem framing, no sales pitch
- **Consideration** → solution comparisons, free trials, demos on request
- **Purchase** → fast sales response, ROI calculators, references

### Conference-Speaker Anti-Pattern

Anyone who has been at a conference where a paid speaker pitches their product instead of educating knows the same lesson. The audience came for awareness-stage content. Selling to them at that moment burns trust for the entire brand.

## Key Takeaway
Match your engagement to the buyer's stage — push selling on Awareness-stage buyers and they're gone forever; ignore Purchase-stage buyers and you lose the deal.

## When to Apply
- Designing landing pages, content, or demo flows
- Setting sales-rep behavior on inbound leads
- Speaking at a conference (don't sell — educate)

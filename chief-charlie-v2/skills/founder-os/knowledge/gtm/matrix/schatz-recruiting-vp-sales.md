## Recruiting Your First VP Sales — Matrix Partners (Stephanie Schatz)

The first VP Sales is almost a co-founder. They will shape the bar and the culture for every sales hire that follows. Mistakes at this stage compound for years. Three practical recommendations:

### 1. Blueprint Great Leaders First

Before launching a search, **meet great sales leaders through your network** — board members, VCs, advisors, friends. Even those who aren't on the market. The point isn't to recruit them; it's to:
- Calibrate what "awesome" looks like at this level (most founders have never met one)
- Sell *yourself* to them — great people know great people, and warm referrals beat retained search 10:1

The interview-skill of recognizing exceptional sales leadership requires having met some. Don't try to evaluate VP Sales candidates without that calibration.

### 2. Run the Retained Search Carefully

Retained search is expensive ($100K-ish) and stakes are high. Three things that improve outcomes:

- **Make the agency emotionally bought in.** Don't just brief them — explain the personal stakes. "I've put my last three years, blood, sweat, and tears into this. I need this person to be extraordinary or this company doesn't work. Help me." Agencies prioritize searches where they care about the founder.
- **Write down very specific criteria, in black and white.** Update them as interviews refine your understanding. The agency's recruiters in the field are different people from your interface — they need a written spec.
- **Be candid and pushy.** Immediate feedback. If the agency is sandbagging or treating you as their #3 client, push back. Bringing a board member into the recruiter conversation can preserve your relationship while pressuring the agency. VC-backed companies have leverage agencies want.

### 3. The Ski Trip Test, Not the Airport Test

Forget the conventional "would I tolerate a 3-hour airport layover with this person?" That's far too low a bar for someone who will shape your culture. Apply the **weekend ski-trip test**: would you spend three days with this person, including evenings? Late-night work sessions, hard times, tough conversations — they all happen. The bar must be high.

### What Profile to Look For

- **Proven sales success** in your specific buyer audience (whatever that is)
- **Low ego** — title doesn't matter, "head of sales" or "director" is fine
- For phase-5 hire: pair the VP with one **Pathfinder Account Executive** with the trailblazer mentality — playbook-creator, not playbook-executor
- Keep it lean: maybe one SDR or CSM, no operations layer, no multi-level sales management

## Key Takeaway
The first VP Sales is co-founder-level — blueprint great ones in your network first, write very specific criteria, and apply the ski-trip test to weed out the merely-tolerable.

## When to Apply
- Founders ready to hire the first sales leader after phase 4 discovery worked
- A previous VP Sales hire failed and you're recovering
- Considering whether to use retained search or your own network

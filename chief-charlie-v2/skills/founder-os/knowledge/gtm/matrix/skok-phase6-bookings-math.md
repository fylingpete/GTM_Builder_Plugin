## The Math Behind Bookings — Phase 6, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

Once a startup is in phase 6, all growth math reduces to a two-factor equation:

```
Bookings = Number of Sales People × PPR (average Productivity Per Rep)
```

Either lever is valid. Both must be tracked separately because they have different drivers and different failure modes.

### Number of Sales People

The most common reason for missing the plan is "we didn't hire sales people fast enough." The remedy is not just an aggressive hiring plan — it is **building an in-house recruiting machine** as a first-class company function. External recruiters can't carry the load at scale.

Growth comes in **discontinuous units** because each new rep ramps over a curve (typical SaaS ramp is 3-6 months to full productivity). Five new hires in Q1 produce limited bookings for two quarters before the cohort comes online. Plans must account for this lag.

### PPR — Productivity Per Rep

Two main drivers of PPR:
- **Quality of sales hires** (right profile, right skills)
- **Onboarding & training** (how fast they ramp to quota)

Plus one often-missed driver: **adequate lead flow**. A rep at quota with no leads will look like a poor performer. Diagnosing low PPR requires checking whether the funnel is starving the rep before blaming the rep.

### Tracking PPR over Time

Track PPR per rep per quarter as a heat-map. Red cells (below target) show whether problems are concentrated in specific reps (hire/coaching issue), specific quarters (seasonality / lead flow), or specific cohorts (onboarding issue).

Two summary metrics for the leadership view:
- **% of Reps above 75% of Quota** — goal: 75%
- **% of Reps above 100% of Quota** — goal: 50%

If either falls, the plan is at risk and the diagnosis must precede any "hire more reps" reflex.

## Key Takeaway
Bookings = Reps × PPR — track both, recognize the ramp lag, and diagnose lead flow before blaming individual reps.

## When to Apply
- Building the next year's sales plan
- Bookings are off-plan and you need to diagnose why
- Considering aggressive sales hiring

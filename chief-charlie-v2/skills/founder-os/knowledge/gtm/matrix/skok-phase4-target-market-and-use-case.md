## Pick One Target Market and One Use Case — Phase 4, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

The output of phase 4 hinges on a single decision: one target market and one use case. Not two, not "primary plus secondary." One.

**Why one is so much better than two:** every additional persona forces marketing to serve multiple audiences, product to build multiple workflows, and services to create multiple offerings. HubSpot's Brian Halligan: "We argued for years about which persona to pick. By not deciding, every team had to serve multiple personas. Once we finally focused on one, every team could focus on attracting and delighting that persona."

**Whole Product reasoning** (from Crossing the Chasm): each use case demands a different set of features, integrations, and partnerships to be a complete solution. Picking one cuts the feature roadmap to manageable size and lets salespeople get smart about that buyer's business fast.

### How to pick

Score candidate target markets on three dimensions:
1. **TAM** — how big is the market?
2. **Pain level / Urgency + Willingness & Ability to pay** — hair-on-fire urgency is essential; without it, sales cycles are long and few deals close
3. **Amount of work to satisfy needs** — high score means *low* work (e.g. an existing buyer pattern, no compliance complexity)

Examples of candidates: Enterprises, Mid-market, SMB, venture-backed hi-tech companies, a specific vertical like financial services. Pick the one where pain and willingness to pay are highest *and* the work to deliver is most manageable.

### Use-Case Messaging Beats Generic Messaging

Compare an AI-powered BI system messaged broadly: "Our software automatically surfaces insights in your data." With a sales-use-case version: "Our Smart Sales Intelligence software automatically tracks all funnel indicators, warns you when any indicator shows a problem, and lets you fix issues early before they affect bookings." The second is dramatically more powerful because it's anchored to a buyer's job-to-be-done.

## Key Takeaway
One target market, one use case, hair-on-fire urgency — picking decisively unlocks every downstream team's ability to win.

## When to Apply
- Early-stage and trying to figure out GTM focus
- Marketing or sales messaging feels generic
- Product backlog is unmanageable because too many personas have asks

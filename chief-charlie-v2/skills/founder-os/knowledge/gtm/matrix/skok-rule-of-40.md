## SaaS Valuations and the Rule of 40 — Phase 8, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

For public SaaS companies, valuation correlates strongly with a single composite metric:

```
Growth Rate (% YoY) + Operating Profit (% of Revenue)
```

This sum is the **Rule of 40**: companies above 40% command premium multiples, companies below 40% are penalized. The intuition is that the market rewards either fast growth or strong profitability, and a healthy SaaS business should produce at least 40% combined.

Examples:
- 70% growth, -20% operating margin = 50 → above the line, premium multiple
- 30% growth, 15% operating margin = 45 → above the line
- 25% growth, 5% operating margin = 30 → below the line, discount multiple

### EV / Gross Profit Beats EV / Revenue

The standard chart most analysts use plots EV / Revenue against Growth + Profitability. Jared Sleeper at Matrix Partners showed that **EV / Gross Profit produces a tighter correlation** because gross margin varies significantly across SaaS companies. A high-growth company with 50% gross margin is fundamentally less valuable than a high-growth company with 80% gross margin even at the same revenue.

The implication: gross margin is a third lever in valuation, not just the cost line. Improving gross margin from 65% to 75% (e.g. by automating customer success, optimizing infrastructure, or reducing services dependency) directly improves valuation in addition to its impact on unit economics.

### How to Use This

1. **Plan to beat the Rule of 40** by your IPO/exit window. The ratio between growth and profitability can shift over time, but the sum should be trending up.
2. **Improve gross margin** as a strategic priority, not just an efficiency project.
3. **Be honest about the trade-off**: hitting 40% with 80% growth and -40% margin requires very strong unit economics to be sustainable; the path to profitability has to be visible.

## Key Takeaway
Growth Rate + Operating Margin should exceed 40% — and improving gross margin is a third valuation lever public investors reward.

## When to Apply
- Preparing for a growth-stage fundraise or IPO
- Choosing between investing in growth versus pulling toward profitability
- Setting next-year operating plan and target operating margin

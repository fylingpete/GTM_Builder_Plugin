## The Revenue Mitochondria — Clone the Pod, Not the Rep — Matrix Partners (Mark Kazanjy)

Once founders successfully prove non-founders can sell (phase 5 → entering phase 6), the next-level abstraction is the cohesive sales unit. Call it the revenue mitochondria — a tight pod that produces revenue as one organism:

- **Sales Development Rep (SDR)** — generates and qualifies pipeline
- **Account Executive (AE)** — closes deals
- **Customer Success Manager (CSM)** — onboards and retains

The unit you scale is **the pod**, not the individual seller. Trying to clone account executives one at a time without their SDR and CSM partners produces reps who can't generate enough pipeline themselves and burn out their accounts post-sale.

### Why the pod beats the lone AE

Each role has a different muscle. SDR work is high-volume outbound — different skill from closing deals. CSM work is post-sale relationship management — different skill from selling. An AE forced to do all three does none well, and produces lower bookings, longer sales cycles, and higher churn.

Once one pod is producing reliable bookings with healthy unit economics, the question shifts from "how do we add another AE?" to **"how do we replicate this pod?"** That includes not just the people, but the processes between them — handoff criteria from SDR to AE, from AE to CSM, the SLAs, the documentation.

### The transition signal

The founder is fully out of selling. Their job is now enabling the system: hiring, onboarding, listening to calls, coaching, and making sure each pod is humming. Founders still parachute in for strategic deals, but day-to-day they're not the closer — they're the architect of the cloning machine.

### Light-touch versions

For higher-velocity / lower-ACV motions, the pod can compress: SDR + AE only, with self-serve onboarding instead of a CSM. For very high ACV motions, expand: add a Solutions Engineer or Implementation Specialist. The principle is the same — match the pod composition to the deal size and motion.

## Key Takeaway
Clone the SDR + AE + CSM pod as a single unit — replicating reps in isolation produces fragile sales orgs that look like they're scaling but aren't.

## When to Apply
- Hired the first AE and trying to figure out what comes next
- Sales is bottlenecked because reps can't generate enough pipeline
- Customer success is overloaded because AEs over-promised

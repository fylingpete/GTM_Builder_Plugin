## Pathfinder/Trailblazer Sales Reps — Phase 4, Matrix Partners (David Skok)

There are two fundamentally different sales-rep profiles, and confusing them costs B2B startups 6-12 months of runway.

**Ordinary sales reps** execute a playbook. They take a written process for a defined ICP — leads come from a known source, the message is tested, the demo is canned, the objections are mapped — and they hit a quota by repeating it. Hiring great ordinary reps requires that the playbook exists.

**Pathfinder/trailblazer reps** are the opposite. They sell into ambiguity. Their job is to *create* the playbook by figuring out:
- Which target market and use case work
- Who in the org to sell to
- What message resonates
- What pricing the market accepts
- What sales motion fits the buyer
- Which product features are missing and block deals

They are comfortable with constant change, work intimately with founders, and care more about pattern-recognition than quota execution.

The biggest sales mistake in phase 4 is hiring ordinary reps before this discovery work is done. Symptoms: the rep ramps slowly, complains about lead quality, blames pricing, and quits or is fired in 6-9 months — taking the customer relationships with them. The honest diagnosis is that the playbook didn't exist; the rep was set up to fail.

Best signals when interviewing for a pathfinder: prior early-stage startup experience where they helped figure out GTM, willingness to spend hours in customer conversations alongside founders, comfort being wrong publicly, and curiosity about the buyer's business rather than commission obsession.

The transition to ordinary reps happens at phase 5 — and only after the pathfinder has produced a playbook a normal rep could execute.

## Key Takeaway
In phase 4 hire pathfinders to create the playbook; in phase 5+ hire ordinary reps to execute it — confusing the two profiles wastes runway.

## When to Apply
- Considering the first sales hire after founder-led selling has worked
- A previously hired rep is struggling and you suspect they were the wrong profile
- Writing the JD for the next sales rep

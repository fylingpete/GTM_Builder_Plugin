## Framework: The Core Four Advertising Methods — Alex Hormozi

Every way to get customers fits into a 2×2 matrix. Understanding this eliminates confusion about "what marketing should I do?"

### The Matrix

Two audience types: **warm** (people who know you) and **cold** (strangers).
Two communication types: **one-to-one** (personal outreach) and **one-to-many** (broadcast).

This gives exactly four methods:

| | One-to-One | One-to-Many |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm** | Warm Outreach | Posting Content |
| **Cold** | Cold Outreach | Paid Ads |

### The Four Methods

**Warm Outreach** — Personally contacting people who already know you. Free, fast, highest conversion rate. Start here.

**Cold Outreach** — Personally contacting strangers. Requires list building and personalization. Lower conversion but unlimited scale.

**Posting Content** — Broadcasting to your existing audience. Free but slow to build. Compounds over time.

**Paid Ads** — Broadcasting to strangers. Expensive but immediately scalable. Requires budget and testing.

### Outbound vs Inbound

Outreach (warm and cold) is outbound — you initiate contact. Content and ads are inbound-generating — they attract people who then reach out to you. Both are necessary. Outbound gives control and speed. Inbound gives scale and leverage.

### Where to Start

Always start with warm outreach. It costs nothing, converts highest, and teaches you how to sell before you spend money on channels that require it. Every other method builds on the skills you develop doing warm outreach.

## Key Takeaway
There are only four ways to get customers — warm outreach, cold outreach, content, and paid ads — start with warm outreach because it's free and fastest.

## When to Apply
- You don't know which marketing channel to focus on
- You're spread across too many channels and none are working
- You're starting from zero and need customers immediately

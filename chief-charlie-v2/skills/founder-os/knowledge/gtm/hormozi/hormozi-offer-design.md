## Framework: 3 Value Vectors + Attraction Offers — Alex Hormozi

When customers can easily compare your offer to competition, you're a commodity. Win on at least one of these three vectors:

### The 3 Value Vectors

1. **Faster** — What would it take to deliver the result in one third of the time? If that means paying contractors 50% more for same-day response, fine — then raise the price to reflect the premium.

2. **Less Risky** — Guarantee against the customer's biggest fear. Options: warranties, guarantees, or an insurance policy on the biggest risk (often cheaper than you'd think).

3. **Easier** — Prepare everything the customer would otherwise do themselves. Intake, onboarding, pre-work. Customers almost always notice and appreciate this effort.

You don't need all three — focus on one and nail it. Then raise your price because you're no longer a commodity. If the current audience can't afford it, go upmarket to people who value speed, risk reduction, and ease.

### Attraction Offers (Packaging)

Wrap your existing offer with:
- **Giveaway** — e.g. "Win a kitchen remodel" (runner-ups get a discount)
- **Win Your Money Back** — great for services requiring active participation
- **Buy X Get Y Free** — give away more free than they pay for (e.g. pay for 4 months, get 8 — adjust price per month accordingly)
- **Decoy Pricing** — works well for transactional businesses

## Key Takeaway
Your lead problem is often an offer problem. If you're a commodity, you get commodity prices and commodity leads. Differentiate through speed, risk, or ease.

## When to Apply
- "Our leads are too expensive" (often it's the offer)
- Price pressure from competition
- Product is easily compared to competitors

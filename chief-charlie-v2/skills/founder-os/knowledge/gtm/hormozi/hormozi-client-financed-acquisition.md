## Framework: Client-Financed Acquisition (CFA) — Alex Hormozi

The biggest constraint on growth is usually cash — you need to spend money to acquire customers, then wait months to recoup it. CFA eliminates this constraint by making every customer pay for themselves on day one.

### The Problem

A $15/month membership with $5 cost produces $10 gross profit per month. With 10-month average retention, LTGP is $100. If CAC is $30, breakeven takes 3 months. During those 3 months, you need cash to fund new acquisitions. Growth is limited by your cash reserves.

### The Solution

Add a one-time upsell at the point of purchase. If you offer a $100 upsell and 20% of buyers take it, that's $20 average additional revenue per customer on day one. Now your day-one revenue ($15 first month + $20 upsell average = $35) exceeds your $30 CAC. You break even immediately.

### Why This Changes Everything

When every customer pays for themselves on day one:
- You can reinvest immediately into acquiring the next customer
- Growth compounds without outside capital
- You are no longer limited by cash reserves
- Competitors who wait 3+ months to breakeven cannot keep pace

### How to Find Upsells

Apply the "more, better, new" hierarchy: sell more of the same (bulk/annual), sell a better version (premium tier), or sell something complementary (related product or service). The best upsells solve the immediate next problem the customer will face after buying.

### The Best Ads Are Free Content

Your highest-performing free content often makes the best ads. User-generated content creates a flywheel — customers create content about your product, which becomes ads, which acquire more customers, who create more content.

## Key Takeaway
Add upsells so day-one revenue covers acquisition cost — when every customer pays for themselves immediately, growth compounds without outside capital.

## When to Apply
- You can't afford to scale ads because cash is tied up in customer acquisition
- Growth is limited by cash flow, not by lead volume
- You need to break even faster on each new customer

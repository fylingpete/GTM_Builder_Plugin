## Framework: Retention as Growth Engine — Alex Hormozi

MRR grows when new members > churned members. Improving retention is often the biggest lever — more effective than acquiring new customers.

### Benchmarks (Monthly Retention)
- Platform average: 80% retention (= 20% churn)
- Good: 90%+ (= <10% churn)
- Bad: <70% (= 30%+ churn) → highest priority to fix

**Critical insight:** Going from 80% to 90% retention is NOT a 10% improvement. It's a **doubling** of customer lifetime value.

### Churn Drops Dramatically with Time
- Month 1-3: 20%+ churn (normal — don't panic)
- After 90 days: drops to ~10% (halved)
- After 6 months: drops to ~2% (5x LTV increase)

**Implication:** Instead of "how do I lower churn?" ask "how do I get customers past day 90?" — that's more tactical and solvable.

### The 3 Critical Timepoints
1. **Month 1** → Activation, onboarding, expectation setting
2. **Day 90** → Deliver a concrete outcome / behavior change
3. **Month 6** → Connections to other customers built

### Two Questions That Clarify Everything
- **To churners:** "Why are you leaving?" → Collect patterns, fix top 2 reasons (review every 20 cancellations)
- **To long-term customers:** "What's the #1 reason you stay?" → Do more of that one thing

## Key Takeaway
Less is often more. If you just do the one thing that makes customers stay better, you don't need five new features. Keeping your promise and removing friction always beats "adding more stuff."

## When to Apply
- Monthly churn rate above 15-20%
- Feeling of "we win customers but lose just as many"
- Recurring revenue business not growing despite new customer acquisition

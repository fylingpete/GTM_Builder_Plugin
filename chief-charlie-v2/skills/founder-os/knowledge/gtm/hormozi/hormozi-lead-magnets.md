## Tactic: Lead Magnets That Convert — Alex Hormozi

Direct-to-offer ads have higher short-term ROAS, but lead magnets have higher long-term ROAS **and** scale better. Reason: direct ads only reach the warmest 5% of the market. Lead magnets reach everyone.

### The 3 Types of Lead Magnets

1. **Reveal a Problem** — "7 mistakes homeowners make when maintaining their HVAC." Shows the customer a problem they didn't know they had.

2. **Trial of the Solution** — Give the customer part of the result for free. Example: an SEO company gets you to position 1-3 in Google Maps for free. Calls in 7 days to ask if you want to keep it. Crushing it.

3. **Splinter Offer (One Step of a Multi-Step Process)** — Pull one component from your full offer and offer it at a steep discount or free. Full offer has components A, B, C, D for $2,000. Take B out, give it for 90% off or free. Customer who has B then wants A, C, D.

### Golden Rule
The more real the lead magnet is (actual cost, actual value), the better it works. Best approach: give away something that others charge for.

### Most Common Mistake
"Request a Quote" on the website is NOT a lead magnet. Nobody wants to request a quote. Give them something in exchange for contact information — something they actually want.

## Key Takeaway
Anyone who says "lead magnets don't work" is thinking too short-term. Long-term and at scale, they beat direct-to-offer significantly.

## When to Apply
- "Request a Quote" as the only conversion on the website
- Leads are too expensive for profitable growth
- Want to scale but direct ads are hitting limits

## Tactic: Breathe the Script — Alex Hormozi

The best salespeople say the script without thinking, the same way you breathe without thinking. This frees 100% of their attention for the prospect.

### Why Memorization Enables Listening

If you're waiting to talk because you're trying to remember the next line, you can't actually hear what the prospect is saying. You miss buying signals, hesitation cues, and opportunities to address concerns. Script mastery is the prerequisite for genuine presence.

### The Drift Problem

A mediocre salesperson closes a deal after accidentally coughing before the close. Next call, they add the cough. Then they add an extra question. Then another. Over time, the script bloats with superstitious habits that got reinforced by coincidence — the prospect would have bought regardless. The best salespeople stay concise. Fewer words per concept means shorter calls, which means more calls per day.

### How to Train It: The Drill Method

An exceptional sales trainer was observed drilling reps on just the opening 30 seconds:

1. Rep delivers the section
2. Trainer gives visual cues and micro-corrections: "Pause harder on this word"
3. Rep does it again with the correction
4. Three correct repetitions in a row before moving on
5. Move to the next section of the script

### The Three Practices for Sales Managers

1. **Rehearse every morning** — daily script practice, non-negotiable
2. **Role play specific sections** — "Today we're focusing on spouse objections only"
3. **One improvement at a time** — you might see a hundred things wrong, but fix one until it's clean, then move to the next

Nobody improves multiple things simultaneously. A coach who says "here are nine things you did wrong" is discouraging and ineffective. The best coaches focus on one thing at a time until all weaknesses are eliminated.

## Key Takeaway
Memorize your script so completely that you can deliver it without thinking — only then can you truly listen to your prospect.

## When to Apply
- Reps sound robotic or keep forgetting parts of the script
- Sales calls are getting longer over time without better results
- Reps can't identify buying signals because they're focused on what to say next

## Framework: LTGP vs CAC — Alex Hormozi

Every business boils down to one comparison: does each customer generate more profit than they cost to acquire?

### The Equation

**LTGP** (Lifetime Gross Profit) = total revenue from a customer minus cost of delivery, over their entire relationship. **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost) = total marketing and sales spend divided by customers acquired.

If LTGP > CAC, you have a business. If LTGP < CAC, you are burning money.

### The Ratios

- **3:1** — Minimum viable ratio. You are profitable but tight. One bad month can flip you negative
- **10:1** — Strong business. Scaling becomes almost automatic because every dollar compounds
- **36:1** — What one portfolio achieved over its lifetime. At this level, growth is limited only by how fast you can deploy capital

### Business Model Beats Ad Skill

Two competitors with identical sales velocity and identical ad spend produced 56x different profit. The difference was not their ads — it was what happened after the sale. Higher LTGP (through upsells, retention, and cross-sells) multiplies every advertising dollar.

### Diagnostic Framework

When growth stalls, diagnose which side is broken:
- Leads coming in but not converting? → Sales problem, not an ad problem
- Customers converting but churning fast? → Product or onboarding problem
- Everything works but margins are thin? → Pricing or cost problem

Don't confuse sales problems with advertising problems. The treatment depends on the correct diagnosis.

## Key Takeaway
Your business model determines profitability more than your ad skill — maximize lifetime gross profit per customer and target at least a 3:1 ratio to CAC.

## When to Apply
- You're spending on ads but don't know if each customer is actually profitable
- CAC feels too high and you can't decide whether to cut spend or increase customer value
- Growth is stalling and you need to diagnose whether it's an acquisition or monetization problem

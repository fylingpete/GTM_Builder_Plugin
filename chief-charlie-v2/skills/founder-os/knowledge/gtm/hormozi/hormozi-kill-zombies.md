## Framework: Kill Zombies Up Front (Obstacles vs. Objections) — Alex Hormozi

An obstacle occurs before you mention the price. An objection occurs after. It's much easier to address concerns before the stakes go up. The best salespeople identify and dismantle obstacles early so the close becomes clean.

### The Three Layers

Every obstacle or objection falls into one of three categories, from surface to core:

1. **Circumstances** (outermost) — Time, money, program fit. Easy to blame, often the default response: "I don't have time," "I can't afford it." These are the easiest to cast blame on.
2. **Other People** — Spouse, business partner, employees. "I would, but my husband won't let me." One step closer to the real decision maker.
3. **Self-Doubt** (innermost) — "I don't think I can do it." When you reach this layer, you're talking to the actual decision maker about their real concern. That's good — now help them decide.

### The Past-to-Future Technique

For each layer, play out the consequence using stories:

**Spouse objection:** Tell about a similar customer who walked out because her husband said no. Years later, nothing changed. She resented him. The reframe: "You're asking for permission when you really need support." One out of three times, the prospect says "Then I'd do it anyway" — and closes right there.

**Time objection:** "Is this seasonal? Will you never be busy again? If you want this to last, start when it's hardest — that's when you'll get the most support."

**Money objection:** "You need resourcefulness, not resources."

### Play It Out Five Years

Take the current trajectory and project it forward. "You've gained five pounds per year. Five years from now, you're 25 pounds heavier. That's the track you're on." Make the cost of inaction visceral and concrete.

### Key Principle

Have memorized stories and metaphors for every common obstacle. Understand the fundamentals behind each so you can adapt — don't just memorize scripts without grasping why they work.

## Key Takeaway
Surface and address every concern before you mention price — obstacles handled early become non-issues, but the same concerns after price becomes objections that kill deals.

## When to Apply
- Deals consistently die at the close despite good rapport
- "I need to talk to my spouse" or "I need to think about it" comes up repeatedly
- You're handling objections reactively instead of proactively

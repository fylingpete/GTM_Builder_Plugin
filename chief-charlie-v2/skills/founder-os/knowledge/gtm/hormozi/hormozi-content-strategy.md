## Framework: 6 Lessons for Audience Building — Alex Hormozi

After 40 months, $4M invested, 35,000 content pieces, and 7.8M new subscribers — these six changes made the biggest difference:

### 1. Education over Edutainment
Entertainment viewers want more entertainment. Education viewers want more education. They don't convert into each other. If you want customers (not just views), educate.

### 2. For the Audience, Not for Yourself
Five tactical changes: clear packaging (thumbnails show exactly what's inside), proof-promise-plan intros, lists/steps/stories over vlogs, effective production over overproduction (words on screen outperform fancy visuals), pre-research over post-production (4 weeks research beats 4 weeks editing).

### 3. Narrow over Broad
Stick to your core topic. Relationship content attracts relationship viewers, not entrepreneurs. Every topic outside the core dilutes the brand.

### 4. Ad Revenue over Views as Metric
Views measure quantity without quality. RPM (revenue per thousand) combines both. Months with the highest RPMs also had the most book sales and applications. A 90-day entertainment experiment: 2-3x more views but ad revenue dropped by half.

### 5. Long-Form over Short-Form
Shorts viewers watch more shorts. Long-form viewers watch more longs. Customers come from long-form. Shorts can lead people to long-form on different platforms, but not within the same one.

### 6. Assume Nothing
Title like they don't know you. Introduce yourself every time. Explain all references. Mentally always talk to a stranger — because if the content does well, you are.

## Key Takeaway
More views ≠ more revenue. The right audience matters more than the biggest audience.

## When to Apply
- Many followers/views but no revenue from them
- Unsure whether to make entertainment or education content
- Content strategy feels aimless

## Framework: GROWTH Onboarding — Alex Hormozi

Most churn happens because customers never experience value. The GROWTH framework ensures every new customer reaches their first win as fast as possible.

### G — Goal Setting
Define what success looks like together with the customer. Not your definition of success — theirs. "What would make this worth it for you?" Their answer becomes the metric you both track.

### R — Resource Allocation
Map out the success path and allocate what they need: tools, access, contacts, materials. Remove friction before they encounter it. Show them exactly what they have available and how to use it.

### O — Optimize First Experience
Engineer a quick win in the first session or first 48 hours. This is the activation moment — once they experience real value, they believe the rest will work. Front-load the easiest, most impressive result.

### W — Workflow Integration
Make the product fit into their existing life or business. If it requires a separate routine, a new login, or a behavior change, reduce that friction. The less they have to change, the more likely they stick.

### T — Track and Provide Feedback
Measure their progress and tell them about it. Customers often do not realize they are making progress without someone pointing it out. Regular check-ins ("You've completed 3 of 5 milestones") reinforce commitment.

### H — Handoff to Ongoing Support
Transition from onboarding to regular support with a clear moment. The customer should know: onboarding is complete, here is who to contact next, and here is what happens from here.

### BAMFAM
Book A Meeting From A Meeting — never end a customer interaction without the next touchpoint scheduled. Customers who drift into no-man's land are the ones who churn.

## Key Takeaway
Engineer a quick win in the first 48 hours, then guide customers through a structured path from goal-setting to ongoing support — never leaving them without a next step.

## When to Apply
- Customers sign up but never use the product
- Churn is highest in the first 30 days
- There is no structured process between sale and ongoing usage

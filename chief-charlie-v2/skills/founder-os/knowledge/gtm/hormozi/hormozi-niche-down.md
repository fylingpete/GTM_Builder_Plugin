## Framework: The 4 Levels of Niching Down — Alex Hormozi

Most founders think niching down is about getting more customers. It is not. Niching is about how much you can charge per customer. The same underlying offer can be worth 100x more when it is narrowed to a specific enough avatar, because the value to that avatar becomes unambiguous and the messaging maps directly to their world.

### The Four Levels (Time Management Example)

1. **Level 1 — Generic topic.** "A course on time management." Price: $19. Competes against every other time management product on the market.
2. **Level 2 — Audience segment.** "A course on time management for salespeople." Price: ~$100. The buyer immediately sees themselves in it.
3. **Level 3 — Audience sub-segment.** "A course on time management for outbound salespeople." Price: ~$2,000. Narrower, sharper, more obviously relevant.
4. **Level 4 — Hyper-specific avatar.** "A system for time management for outbound sales reps who sell B2B power tools to gardening companies." Price: high four figures or more. At this level, the value of a single sale is known to the buyer — so the price of the solution maps cleanly to that value.

The fundamentals of all four systems are essentially identical. The difference in price between Level 1 and Level 4 is roughly 100x. And you will not get 100x the volume by staying broad, because at Level 1 you are undifferentiated. The pricing power comes from the specificity, not from the work.

### The Expansion Path

Start narrow, then expand outward as demand and reputation grow. Most successful educators follow this arc: master a tight niche first, build a reputation there, then widen the circle one step at a time. You expand when the questions you keep getting naturally point to the next adjacent audience — not before.

The rule for expansion: **never make an adjacent move for a 2x. Only make it for a 10x.** Broader markets are tempting but they dilute your pricing power. Only expand when the opportunity created by the move more than replaces your current revenue at the current niche.

## Key Takeaway
Niche for pricing power, not volume — the same offer is worth 100x more to a hyper-specific avatar, so start as narrow as you can master, then expand outward only when the next move is a 10x, not a 2x.

## When to Apply
- Your offer feels generic and you are competing on price
- You cannot charge what you want because buyers do not see themselves in the offer
- You have a broad audience but low conversion and low average order value
- You are tempted to expand into an adjacent market and need a rule for when to do it
- You are starting out and unsure how narrow is too narrow

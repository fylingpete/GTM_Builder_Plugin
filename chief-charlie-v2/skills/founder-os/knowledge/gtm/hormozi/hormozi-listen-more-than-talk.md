## Tactic: Listen More Than You Talk — Alex Hormozi

AI analysis of thousands of sales calls across a large portfolio revealed a clear pattern: the best salespeople listen twice as much as they talk. The worst salespeople are the ones who don't shut up.

### Why Listening Closes

Prospects believe nothing you say. They believe everything *they* say. When you ask questions and let them talk, they sell themselves. When you answer questions, you give them something to attack and disagree with.

The person answering questions is the one getting interrogated. You don't want to be that person.

### Alignment Selling

When selling weight loss, pushing a personal agenda of what people "should" do failed. There wasn't time in 15 minutes to re-educate someone on their last 10 years of decisions. What worked: asking what they'd done before, what they liked, what they didn't. Then highlighting only the features of the offer that matched what they already wanted.

"Tell me why you're different" → "What have you done in the past?" → "What did you like?" → "What didn't you like?" → Now you know which piano keys to play. The prospect hears exactly what they wanted and thinks: "This is perfect."

### Pattern Recognition Over Pushing

Brilliant salespeople sense when someone isn't ready. Instead of forcing the close, they ask more questions: "I feel some hesitation. What are your main concerns right now?" Never ask for the sale unless you already know they're going to buy — just like lawyers never ask questions on the stand they don't already know the answer to.

### Break Patterns With Commands

Instead of "How's it going?" (automatic response), try "Tell me about your day." Commands break autopilot and make people more present.

## Key Takeaway
Let the prospect tell you what they want, then show them you have exactly that — they'll close themselves.

## When to Apply
- Sales calls feel confrontational or one-sided
- Prospects push back on features you're highlighting
- You're talking more than 40% of the call time

## Framework: On-Call / Off-Call SOPs + BAMFAM — Alex Hormozi

Sales output is directly correlated with opportunities created between calls. Yet most companies only train what to say *on* the call and ignore everything in between.

### Two Checklists, Two Hats

**On-Call SOP** — what you say when talking to prospects. Your script, your close, your objection handling.

**Off-Call SOP** — what you do the second you hang up. The best salespeople switch hats immediately and work a checklist:
- Follow up with pending prospects
- Send personalized reach-outs (voice memos, video messages)
- Drag forward appointments from later in the week
- Cancel or reschedule no-shows
- Send pre-call engagement ("What size shirt do you want?" / "Which gift card do you prefer?")

The tiny engagement questions work because the prospect thinks: "This person went out of their way — the least I can do is show up."

### BAMFAM: Book a Meeting from a Meeting

Never end a call without knowing when you'll talk next. If a prospect says "let's circle back," address it immediately — both calendars are open right now.

If they resist scheduling, that's an obstacle worth addressing: "Isn't this a problem for you? How much are you losing every day not implementing this? Why wouldn't we move sooner?"

Letting a prospect float into no man's land without a next meeting is how leads die.

### Why Most Companies Lose Money Here

The vast majority of companies don't have an off-call SOP. They just say "work the leads in the meantime." But sales output is so tightly correlated with opportunity volume that the time between calls is where the real leverage sits.

## Key Takeaway
The time between calls is where most salespeople lose — build a structured off-call SOP and never end a call without the next meeting booked.

## When to Apply
- Reps have downtime between calls but aren't generating new opportunities
- Leads go cold because no one follows up between scheduled calls
- Show rates are low and prospects ghost after the first conversation

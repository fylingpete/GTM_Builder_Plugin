## Framework: Ad Anatomy (Call-Out, Value, CTA) — Alex Hormozi

Every effective ad has exactly three parts. Missing any one of them kills performance.

### 1. Call-Out — Get Noticed

Your ad competes with everything else on the screen. The call-out's job is to make your target audience stop and pay attention. Four types:

- **Labels** — "Hey, gym owners" or "Attention SaaS founders." Directly name your audience
- **Yes questions** — "Tired of cold leads that never close?" If they say yes mentally, they keep reading
- **If-then statements** — "If you run a service business doing $50K+/month, this is for you"
- **Ridiculous results** — A headline so compelling it demands attention

Visual call-outs work through contrast (stand out from the feed), likeness (look like your audience), and scene (show the desired outcome).

### 2. Value Content — The What-Who-When

The body of the ad delivers value using the what-who-when framework. What: cover all four value equation drivers plus their opposites. Who: show benefits from multiple perspectives — spouse, kids, boss, rivals. When: span past, present, and future timelines.

### 3. Call to Action — Clear, Not Clever

Tell them exactly what to do next. Simple URLs, short domains, one clear next step. No ambiguity. "Click the link below to book a free call" beats "Learn more about our revolutionary approach."

### The Testing Imperative

Facebook data shows the top 0.1% of advertisers test 11x more creative variations than everyone else. The difference between good and great advertising is volume of testing, not a single brilliant idea.

"Assume the audience has no idea who you are, what you do, or how it works. They're in a rush and have a third-grade education."

## Key Takeaway
Every ad needs a call-out to stop the scroll, value content to earn attention, and a clear CTA — then test 11x more variations than you think you need.

## When to Apply
- Your ads get impressions but low click-through rates
- You don't know how to structure ad copy
- You're running the same creative for months without testing alternatives

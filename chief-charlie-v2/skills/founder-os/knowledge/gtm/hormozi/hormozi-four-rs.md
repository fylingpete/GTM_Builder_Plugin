## Framework: The 4 R's (Post-Sale Maximization) — Alex Hormozi

Most entrepreneurs focus entirely on getting the sale and forget what happens after. The 4 R's framework ensures you extract maximum value from every customer interaction.

### The 4 R's

1. **Review** — Get a testimonial or written review immediately while enthusiasm is highest. This becomes proof for future prospects. The longer you wait, the less likely they are to do it
2. **Referral** — Ask who else could benefit. The moment after purchase is when belief in the product peaks. Use it
3. **Retain** — Keep them as a customer longer. Every additional month of retention increases lifetime value without any acquisition cost
4. **Resell** — Offer the next product or service. If they bought the starter package, show them the premium. If they bought one-time, offer recurring

### Hidden Costs: Why People Don't Buy

When someone doesn't purchase despite the price being "right," hidden costs are at work. These are the inverse of the value equation:

- **Effort** — How much work does the customer have to put in?
- **Sacrifice** — What do they have to give up to participate?
- **Risk** — What happens if it doesn't work?
- **Time delay** — How long until they see results?

Reducing these hidden costs often matters more than lowering the price. A customer will pay more for something that requires less effort, less sacrifice, lower risk, and faster results.

Execute the 4 R's systematically after every sale and you create a compounding machine where each customer generates multiple additional revenue streams.

## Key Takeaway
The sale is not the finish line — it is the starting line for reviews, referrals, retention, and reselling that multiply the value of every customer.

## When to Apply
- Customers buy once and never come back
- You have no testimonials or social proof despite having customers
- You are not asking for referrals after each sale

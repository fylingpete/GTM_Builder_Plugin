## Tactic: Best Leads to Best Closers — Alex Hormozi

Most companies distribute leads randomly or round-robin. This wastes your highest-quality opportunities on your lowest-performing reps.

### The Speed Advantage

Data shows that responding to a lead within 60 seconds generates 4x the revenue compared to delayed response. The first company to make meaningful contact wins the deal more often than the company with the better product. Speed to contact is the single highest-leverage sales metric.

### The Routing Principle

Your best leads should go to your best closers. Period. Not round-robin, not whoever is next in queue. Route by lead quality × rep performance.

### The Timeshare Discovery

One company 5x'd their revenue by making a simple change: they stopped distributing leads evenly and started sending the hottest leads to their top closer. The top rep's close rate on premium leads was so much higher than average that the reallocation alone transformed the business.

### Survivorship Bias Warning

When junior reps only get the harder, lower-quality leads, they struggle and appear incompetent — even if they are good. This creates a false signal that "we can't find good salespeople" when the real issue is lead routing. Give junior reps a mix of quality levels so they can build confidence and skills.

### Tracking

Automate metrics tracking by rep, platform, and product. You need to know not just who closes most, but who closes best from which source on which offer. This data drives intelligent routing decisions.

## Key Takeaway
Stop distributing leads randomly — route your best opportunities to your best closers and respond within 60 seconds.

## When to Apply
- Your best leads are going to reps who can't close them
- Speed to contact is slow and leads go cold
- Top reps and bottom reps get the same quality leads

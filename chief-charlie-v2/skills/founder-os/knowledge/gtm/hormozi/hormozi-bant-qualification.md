## Framework: BANT Qualification — Alex Hormozi

Not every lead deserves a full sales conversation. BANT helps you identify in the first few minutes whether a prospect can and will buy — so you stop wasting hours on people who were never going to close.

### The Four Qualifiers

**Budget** — Can they afford it? This is not about whether they think it's expensive. It's about whether they have the financial means to pay. Ask early: "Is this something you've budgeted for?" or "What have you invested in solutions like this before?"

**Authority** — Are they the decision maker? If the person on the phone needs to "check with my boss/partner/spouse" before committing, you are talking to the wrong person. Either get the decision maker on the call or disqualify.

**Need** — Do they actually have the problem you solve? Sometimes people are curious but not in pain. Curiosity does not close. Need does.

**Timing** — Are they ready to act now? A prospect with budget, authority, and need but no urgency will drag on for months. Ask: "If we could solve this, when would you want to start?"

### Implementation

Outsource appointment setting and calendaring so your closers only speak to qualified prospects. For B2B, a proven voicemail tactic: reference the prospect's competitor by name. "Hi John, calling in reference to [competitor name]. Call me back at..." This gets callbacks because nobody wants to miss what their competitor is doing.

### When to Disqualify

If a prospect fails on two or more BANT criteria, politely move on. Your time is better spent on someone who scores high on all four.

## Key Takeaway
Qualify every prospect on Budget, Authority, Need, and Timing in the first minutes of a conversation — then invest your selling time only in those who pass.

## When to Apply
- You spend hours on calls that never close
- Prospects say yes to everything but never actually buy
- You need to prioritize a large pipeline with limited selling time

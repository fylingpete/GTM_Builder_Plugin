## Tactic: Cold Outreach That Gets Responses — Alex Hormozi

Three strangers problems: you can't reach them, they ignore you, and they're not interested. Solve all three with personalization, simplicity, and overwhelming value.

### Personalize Like You Know Them

Reference something specific — their company, a recent post, their industry challenge. The goal is that each message reads like it was written for one person, even if you send hundreds. Thirty seconds of research per contact transforms response rates.

### Write Below Third-Grade Reading Level

Simple language gets a 50% response bump over complex language. Short sentences. Common words. No jargon. No paragraph-long explanations. If a 9-year-old can't understand it, simplify.

### Big, Fast Value

Give away what others charge for. If competitors sell audits, give yours free. If competitors charge for the first consultation, do it at no cost. This creates a 3x improvement in response rates because the prospect gets genuine value with zero risk.

### The Volume Game

Match your best-performing rep's process, double their volume, then innovate. Don't innovate before you have volume data. The voicemail script that gets callbacks: "Hey [name], calling in reference to [their competitor]. Call me back at..." Nobody wants to miss what their competitor is doing.

### Benchmarks

100 calls per day. 25% pickup rate. 25% of those take the lead magnet. That equals 4 engaged leads per day — reliable, scalable, and invisible to competitors (they can't see your outreach like they can see your ads).

### Follow Up Relentlessly

After the full list is exhausted, follow up more times in more ways — email, call, text, social. Then wait 3-6 months and restart the entire list.

## Key Takeaway
Personalize every message, write at a third-grade level, and give away what competitors charge for — then do it 100 times a day.

## When to Apply
- Cold emails and calls get no responses
- You're sending generic outreach and wondering why it doesn't work
- You need a reliable lead source that competitors can't copy or see

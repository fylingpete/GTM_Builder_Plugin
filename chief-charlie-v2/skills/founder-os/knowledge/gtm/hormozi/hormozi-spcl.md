## Framework: SPCL — The 4 Pillars of Influence — Alex Hormozi

Influence is not follower count. Influence is the likelihood that someone does what you suggest. It's built from four elements:

### S — Status (Control Over Scarce Resources)
Whoever controls something others want has status. A bartender has status behind the bar — not outside it. In content: demonstrate that you have access to something valuable.

### P — Power (Say-Do Correspondence)
When you say "do X," the person does it, and something good happens — your power with that person increases. The more cycles like this, the more influence. This is why parents have massive power: thousands of reinforcement cycles since childhood.

In content: give actionable instructions → when the viewer executes and it works, your power grows.

### C — Credibility (Third-Party Confirmation)
Not you saying you're good — others confirming it. Verified results, testimonials, certifications, third-party validation.

In content: proof in the intro. Numbers, outcomes, references.

### L — Likeness (Similarity)
People follow people who are similar to them — visually, in values, or in behavior. A woman talking about business has ~54% female audience, a man ~89% male audience.

In content: be yourself. Zero ROI in pretending to be someone else.

### Why Long-Form > Short-Form for Influence
In 30 seconds: barely any reinforcement cycles possible. In 2 hours: hundreds. Someone would need to watch ~480 shorts to get the same exposure as 2 hours of long-form. That's why livestreamers have more influence than shorts creators.

## Key Takeaway
Every piece of content should demonstrate at least one of the four elements. All four together = maximum influence. More influence = higher compliance with every request you make.

## When to Apply
- Many followers but nobody buys
- Content feels "empty" — entertaining but no impact
- Want to build authority in a specific topic

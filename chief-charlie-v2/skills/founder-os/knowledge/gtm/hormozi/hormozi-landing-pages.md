## Tactic: Landing Page Simplicity — Alex Hormozi

The best-converting landing pages are embarrassingly simple. Complexity kills conversion.

### The Formula

**Headline** — Restates the promise from the ad. If the ad said "Double your leads in 30 days," the landing page headline should echo that language. Consistency between ad and page is called "message match" — breaking it confuses visitors and drops conversion.

**Sub-headline** — Adds one layer of detail. Addresses the most common objection or clarifies who this is for. "For service businesses doing $10K+/month who want predictable lead flow."

**Form** — Name, email, phone. That's it. Every additional field reduces submissions. Fewer steps = more leads. More steps = fewer but more qualified leads. At early stage, optimize for volume.

### Design Rules

**Match the ad visually.** Same colors, same imagery style, same energy. The visitor should feel like the landing page is a continuation of the ad, not a different destination.

**Screenshots beat graphics.** Product screenshots, real results, actual interfaces. Professionally designed illustrations look nice but communicate less trust than a raw screenshot of real data.

**Short domains.** A short, memorable URL improves both click-through from ads and direct-type traffic. If your domain is long or complicated, get a short redirect.

### The Qualification Trade-Off

Fewer steps captures more leads but less qualified ones. More steps (additional questions, qualification forms) captures fewer leads but better ones. There is no universal right answer — it depends on your sales capacity and lead cost.

## Key Takeaway
Headline + sub-headline + form, matching the ad's colors and words — simplicity converts, complexity confuses.

## When to Apply
- Your landing page has a high bounce rate despite good ad performance
- You're losing people between the ad click and the form submission
- Your landing page has too many elements and you don't know what to cut

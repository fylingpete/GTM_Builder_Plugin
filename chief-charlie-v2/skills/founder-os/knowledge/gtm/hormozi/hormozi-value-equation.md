## The Value Equation — Alex Hormozi

Perceived value is not a vague feeling — it follows a formula with four distinct variables. The numerator contains what people want. The denominator contains what they want to avoid.

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)**

### The Four Variables

1. **Dream Outcome** — The more meaningful the end result, the more people will pay. Frame the outcome in terms of what matters most to the customer.

2. **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** — Customers pay a premium for certainty. A surgeon with 10,000 successful procedures commands a far higher fee than one performing their first. Social proof, track record, and guarantees all increase this variable.

3. **Time Delay** — Speed beats free. FedEx charges a massive premium over standard postal service for the same action — moving a package from A to B. The faster you deliver the result, the more valuable it becomes.

4. **Effort and Sacrifice** — Minimize what the customer must endure. CoolSculpting charges thousands for fat removal requiring no exercise, no dieting, and no recovery. A gym membership costs $30 a month but demands enormous ongoing effort.

### Driving the Denominator to Zero

The most valuable companies focus relentlessly on reducing the denominator. One-click purchasing, instant streaming, same-day delivery — each pushes time delay and effort closer to zero. When the denominator approaches zero, perceived value approaches infinity.

### Perception Is Reality

Delivering value is not enough — you must communicate it. A product that works but feels difficult will be valued less than one that works and feels effortless. Design your messaging and experience around all four variables.

## Key Takeaway
Maximize dream outcome and perceived likelihood while driving time delay and effort as close to zero as possible — that is how you create offers people cannot refuse.

## When to Apply
- You deliver great results but customers still resist your price
- You want a systematic way to increase perceived value without adding cost
- You are redesigning your offer and need to decide where to invest effort

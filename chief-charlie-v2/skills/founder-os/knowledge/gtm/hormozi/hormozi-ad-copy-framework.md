## Framework: What-Who-When Ad Copy — Alex Hormozi

Most ad copy covers one benefit from one perspective at one point in time. The What-Who-When framework multiplies your angles exponentially.

### What — 8 Elements

Take the four value equation drivers and their opposites:
1. **Dream outcome** — What they'll achieve
2. **Perceived likelihood** — Why it will work for them
3. **Reduced time delay** — How fast they'll see results
4. **Reduced effort** — How easy the process is
5. **Nightmare avoided** — What happens if they don't act
6. **Risk eliminated** — How they're protected from failure
7. **Slowness punished** — Cost of waiting
8. **Difficulty highlighted** — Pain of the current hard way

Each element is a different ad angle. Eight angles from one offer.

### Who — Status-Driven Perspectives

Show the benefit from multiple viewpoints:
- How their **spouse** will see them differently
- How their **kids** will benefit
- How their **boss** will react
- How their **rivals** will respond
- How their **colleagues** will perceive them

People buy for status as much as function. Showing social impact makes the benefit feel more real and urgent.

### When — Timeline

For each benefit, span three timeframes:
- **Past** — "Remember when you used to struggle with..."
- **Present** — "Right now, you're dealing with..."
- **Future** — "Imagine six months from now..."

### The Multiplication

8 what-elements × 5 who-perspectives × 3 when-timelines = 120 unique ad angles from a single offer. You will never run out of creative variations.

## Key Takeaway
Multiply your ad angles using What (8 value elements) × Who (5 social perspectives) × When (3 timelines) — one offer generates 120 unique creative directions.

## When to Apply
- You've been running the same ad copy and it's fatiguing
- You don't know what to say in your ads beyond the basic pitch
- You need more creative variations to test

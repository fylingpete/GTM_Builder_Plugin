## Premium Pricing: The Virtuous Cycle — Alex Hormozi

There are only two ways to improve the gap between what you charge and the value customers perceive. You can lower the price, or you can raise the value. Lowering the price is the path most people take, and it leads to failure. Raising the value has infinite upside.

### The Virtuous Cycle of High Prices

When you charge more, customers invest more emotionally. That investment raises their perceived value. Higher perceived value leads to better engagement and results. Better results give you stronger case studies, which increases your conviction. Greater conviction lets you sell with more authority and charge even more. The cycle compounds.

### The Vicious Cycle of Low Prices

Lowering prices triggers the opposite spiral. Lower revenue erodes margin, forcing you to cut quality. Worse quality leads to worse results. Worse results destroy conviction and make selling harder. You lower the price again. Eventually, the business dies.

### Price as a Signal

Price is not just a number — it is a communication tool. In blind wine studies, people rate identical wine significantly higher when told it costs more. The price itself changes the experience. When something costs more, people take it more seriously, follow through more diligently, and report greater satisfaction.

### Category of One Pricing

The strategic goal is to be so expensive that prospects cannot place you in the same mental category as competitors. When someone quotes $500 and you quote $50,000, the prospect does not compare — they assume you sell something fundamentally different.

## Key Takeaway
Raising prices creates a self-reinforcing loop of better results, stronger proof, and deeper conviction — while lowering prices starts a death spiral.

## When to Apply
- You feel pressure to discount and are afraid of losing customers to cheaper alternatives
- Your margins are thin and you struggle to invest in quality or marketing
- You want to reposition from budget provider to premium category leader

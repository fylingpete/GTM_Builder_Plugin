## Framework: The CLOSER Sales Script — Alex Hormozi

A structured sales conversation that guides prospects from problem awareness to confident purchase. Each letter represents a phase of the call.

### C — Clarify Why They're Here

Start by understanding their situation. Why did they book the call? What prompted them to reach out now? This gives you context and makes them feel heard.

### L — Label the Problem

Repeat their problem back to them in clear language. When you articulate their pain better than they can, trust skyrockets. "So what you're saying is..."

### O — Overview Past Attempts

Ask what they have tried before and why it didn't work. This reveals their objections before they voice them and shows you which approaches to avoid positioning against.

### S — Sell the Vacation, Not the Plane

Nobody wants a plane ride — they want the destination. Describe the outcome, not the process. Paint the picture of life after the problem is solved. The three-pillar pitch helps: like a stool with three legs, show three components that together create the result. Remove any one leg and the stool falls.

### E — Explain the Solution

Now — and only now — explain how your offer specifically addresses their situation. Map your solution to the specific problems they mentioned in the Clarify and Overview phases.

### R — Reinforce the Decision

After they buy, reinforce that they made the right choice. Buyer's remorse kills retention. Tell them what happens next, what to expect in the first 48 hours, and why they are going to succeed.

### Handling Objections

Objections fall into five categories: time ("not now"), stall ("let me think about it"), money ("can't afford it"), preferences ("not what I expected"), and authority ("need to ask my spouse/partner"). Each requires a different approach.

## Key Takeaway
Follow the CLOSER sequence to guide prospects naturally from their problem to your solution — sell the destination, not the vehicle.

## When to Apply
- Your sales calls feel unstructured and outcomes are inconsistent
- You close some deals but can't replicate why certain calls work
- New salespeople need a framework they can follow immediately

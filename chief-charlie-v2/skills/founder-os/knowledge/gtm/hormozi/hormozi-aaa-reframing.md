## Framework: AAA Reframing (Acknowledge, Associate, Ask) — Alex Hormozi

The difference between top closers and the rest: top closers ask for the sale more often — without the customer feeling pressured. The AAA framework makes this possible.

### 1. Acknowledge
Repeat what they said back. This shows active listening and buys you time to think.
> "So you're curious about what certifications we have?"

### 2. Associate
Connect their question/objection with the behavior of successful customers.
> "That's a great question. It actually shows you're making a serious decision here."

Three variants:
- **Straw man:** "Someone asked me the exact same thing earlier today. Can I share what I told them?"
- **Successful customer:** "That makes you just like Sarah, who had a similar business and crushed it."
- **Appeal to authority:** "My CEO just told me something about this today. Can I share it?"

### 3. Ask
Ask a counter-question instead of answering directly.
> "Which certifications are you looking for specifically?"

### The 5 Rules

1. **Customers believe themselves, not you** — lead them to their own conclusions via questions
2. **Be like smoke** — never disagree, always sidestep with questions. You can't disagree with a question
3. **Tell them what their question means** — frame the objection positively before responding
4. **Use straw men for tough truths** — talk about a third party instead of confronting directly
5. **Retain childlike curiosity** — "Huh, that's interesting. Tell me more" instead of getting defensive

### Universal Questions for Any Objection
- "What's your main concern?"
- "What would make this a no?"
- "What would it take for you to say yes?"
- "What are the variables you're using to make this decision?"

## Key Takeaway
Never answer a question directly. Ask questions about the question. Whoever asks the questions controls the conversation — and with the AAA framework you stay likeable instead of aggressive.

## When to Apply
- Customer says "I need to think about it" or "my partner needs to agree"
- Objections feel like dead ends
- Sales conversations become confrontational instead of cooperative
- Want to increase close rate without adding more pressure

## Framework: Cohort Analysis for Churn Prevention — Alex Hormozi

Aggregate churn rates hide the real story. A 5% monthly churn rate could mean everyone leaves gradually or that one specific month is a cliff. Cohort analysis reveals the truth.

### How It Works

Group customers by the month they signed up. Track each group's retention month by month. Plot it. You'll see patterns: maybe month 3 is where most people drop off, or maybe month 7 is a second cliff.

### The Preemptive Strike

Once you know when churn happens, add incentives 1-2 months before the drop-off point. If month 3 is the cliff, create a special engagement, bonus, or check-in at month 2 that re-anchors commitment before doubt sets in.

### Segment by Product Pathway

Different products create different retention curves. A customer who enters through your lead magnet, then buys the starter package, then upgrades to premium may retain completely differently than one who jumps straight to premium. Map these pathways and identify which sequence produces the highest lifetime value.

### Separate Product Pipelines

At this stage, each product should have its own improvement pipeline with a dedicated owner. The person responsible for the core product should not also be responsible for the premium tier — they have different customers, different problems, and different metrics.

### Incremental Improvement

Product improvement at this stage is about optimization, not reinvention. Small, consistent improvements compound. Measure everything before and after. The goal is to make the existing product incrementally better, not to build something new.

## Key Takeaway
Group customers by signup month, find the churn cliff, then add incentives 1-2 months before it — prevention beats recovery.

## When to Apply
- You know your churn rate but don't know when or why customers leave
- Retention efforts feel random rather than targeted
- Different product entry paths seem to produce different customer quality

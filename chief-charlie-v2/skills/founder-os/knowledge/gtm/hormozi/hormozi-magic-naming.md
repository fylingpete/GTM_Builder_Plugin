## Framework: MAGIC Naming Formula — Alex Hormozi

A product name is the first thing a prospect encounters, and a weak name kills interest before the offer even gets a chance. The MAGIC formula provides five elements to build names that attract attention and communicate value. Aim to include three or four of these in every name.

1. **M — Magnetic Reason Why** — Give people a compelling reason to pay attention. This could be a bold claim, a counterintuitive hook, or a specific result.
2. **A — Announce the Avatar** — Make it clear who this is for. The right audience should immediately self-identify when they see the name.
3. **G — Give Them a Goal** — State the outcome or destination. People want to know what they will achieve, not just what they will learn.
4. **I — Indicate a Time Interval** — Shorter time frames create higher perceived value. A "21-Day" program feels more valuable than an "8-Week" program even if the content is identical, because it implies faster results.
5. **C — Complete with a Container Word** — Use a word that implies a specific delivery format: challenge, blueprint, bootcamp, system, accelerator, masterclass. The container word sets expectations about the experience.

People want their problem solved in a specific way. The name communicates both the outcome and the delivery method, which is why it matters so much for conversion.

When promotions fatigue — and they will — there is a refresh hierarchy ranked from least to most effort: swap the creative images, rewrite the body copy, change the headline using the MAGIC formula, adjust the duration metric, change the lead enhancer (free, one dollar, ninety-nine percent off), or restructure the monetization model entirely. In local or niche markets where audiences are small, promotions fatigue fast. Plan to refresh roughly every twenty-one days.

## Key Takeaway
Use the MAGIC formula to build product names that magnetically attract the right audience by combining a reason, an avatar, a goal, a time interval, and a container word.

## When to Apply
- Your product name is generic and does not stand out in a crowded market
- Promotions are losing effectiveness and you need to refresh without rebuilding the offer
- You are launching something new and want the name itself to drive curiosity and clicks

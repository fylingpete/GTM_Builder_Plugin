## Tactic: Warm Outreach with the ACA Framework — Alex Hormozi

Warm outreach means personally contacting people who already know you — friends, former colleagues, social media connections. It is the fastest, cheapest way to get your first customers.

### The Step-by-Step Process

1. **Get your list** — Export contacts from phone, email, and social media. You have more connections than you think
2. **Pick a platform** — Choose whichever you are most active on (Instagram DMs, text, LinkedIn, email)
3. **Personalize** — Spend 30 seconds researching each person. Reference something specific about them
4. **Reach out to 100 people per day** — Volume matters. Most won't respond. That's normal
5. **Follow up 3 times** — If no response, follow up at day 2, day 5, and day 10. Most sales happen on follow-ups, not initial contact

### The ACA Framework

**Acknowledge** — Reference something personal about them. A recent post, a life event, a shared memory. This proves you are not mass-messaging.

**Compliment** — Associate a positive character trait with them. "You seem like someone who..." or "I've always admired your..." People respond to character labels — they want to live up to them.

**Ask** — Ask the next natural question that leads the conversation toward your offer. Don't pitch immediately. Engage first, then transition.

### The Three Rewards

People respond to three things: attention ("I noticed you"), affection ("I care about you"), and approval ("I admire you"). The ACA framework delivers all three in the first message.

### Indirect Pitch

Don't pitch to them directly. Instead: "Do you know anybody who might benefit from...?" This removes pressure and often results in them saying "Actually, I might be interested."

## Key Takeaway
Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask — then reach out to 100 people per day and follow up three times each.

## When to Apply
- You need your first customers and have no marketing budget
- Cold outreach feels too intimidating to start with
- You have a network but haven't leveraged it for business

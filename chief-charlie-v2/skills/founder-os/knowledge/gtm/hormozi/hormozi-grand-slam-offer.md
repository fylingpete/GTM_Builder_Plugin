## Grand Slam Offer — Alex Hormozi

A Grand Slam Offer is so differentiated that prospects cannot compare you to anyone else. When you achieve this, the buying decision shifts from price-based to value-based. Instead of asking "who is cheapest?", the prospect asks "how do I get this?"

The compounding effect makes this powerful. A better offer improves every metric simultaneously: higher click-through rates on ads, higher close rates in sales conversations, and the ability to charge premium prices. These multiply together for exponential revenue growth rather than incremental gains.

### Real-World Examples

A weight-loss business restructured from a $99/month subscription to a $600 upfront challenge format. The result was a 30:1 return on ad spend. An agency shifted from a flat retainer model to pay-per-performance pricing. Their ROAS jumped from 0.5:1 to 11:1 — same service, radically different offer structure.

### The 9 Components

A complete Grand Slam Offer draws on nine building blocks:

1. **Starving Crowd** — pick a market with desperate demand
2. **Pricing** — charge what the transformation is worth
3. **Value Equation** — maximize perceived value across four variables
4. **Problem-Solution Mapping** — address every obstacle in the customer journey
5. **Delivery Vehicles** — choose the right format for each solution
6. **Bonuses** — stack additional value that reduces risk
7. **Guarantees** — remove the prospect's downside
8. **Scarcity and Urgency** — create real reasons to act now
9. **Naming** — package everything under a compelling name

Each component reinforces the others. The goal is an offer so compelling it feels irresponsible to say no.

## Key Takeaway
A Grand Slam Offer makes you incomparable — when prospects cannot shop you against alternatives, you escape price competition entirely.

## When to Apply
- You are stuck competing on price and margins are shrinking
- Your ads generate clicks but few conversions — the offer itself is the bottleneck
- You want to restructure an existing service into a premium, high-conversion package

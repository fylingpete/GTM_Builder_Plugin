## Framework: Three Phases of Ad Spend — Alex Hormozi

Most entrepreneurs quit advertising during the learning phase, thinking it "doesn't work." Understanding the three phases prevents this fatal mistake.

### Phase 1: Track Your Money (Learn)

Before optimizing anything, know where every dollar goes. Set up tracking so you can see: cost per click, cost per lead, cost per acquisition, and lifetime value per customer. Without tracking, you are flying blind and every opinion about your ads is a guess.

### Phase 2: Lose Money (Invest in Data)

This is where most people quit. Early ad campaigns lose money. This is not failure — it is tuition. You are buying data about what works and what doesn't. Every failed ad teaches you something about your audience, your offer, or your creative.

The mistake is quitting before you have enough data to draw conclusions. Running 100 emails and getting zero customers does not mean email doesn't work. It means you don't have enough data yet.

### Phase 3: Print Money (Scale What Works)

Once you find a winning combination of audience, creative, and offer, scale it. Increase budget gradually, monitor efficiency, and keep testing new creative to prevent fatigue. At this phase, every dollar in produces multiple dollars out — predictably.

### The Critical Insight

Most founders send too few outreach messages or run too small a test budget, get zero results, and conclude that the channel doesn't work. The real conclusion: you didn't do enough to generate meaningful data. Phase 2 requires patience and budget before Phase 3 becomes possible.

## Key Takeaway
Expect to lose money learning what works — the businesses that survive Phase 2 get to print money in Phase 3.

## When to Apply
- You tried ads once, lost money, and stopped
- You don't know whether your ad spend is an investment or a waste
- You want to scale ads but are afraid of losing money

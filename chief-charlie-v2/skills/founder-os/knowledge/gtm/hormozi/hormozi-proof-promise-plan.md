## Tactic: Proof-Promise-Plan for Video Intros — Alex Hormozi

From analyzing the top 35 educational videos, a consistent intro pattern emerged:

### 1. Proof
Prove you know what you're talking about. For entertainers this means showing the spectacle. For educators it means showing your results or credentials.

> Example: "In the last 12 months, my social media had 3 billion impressions and brought in 4.5 million new subscribers."

### 2. Promise
Tell the viewer exactly what they'll get or learn.

> Example: "I'll show you six changes that made money and grew my brand — that you can use today."

### 3. Plan
Set expectations for what happens next.

> Example: "We'll walk through six changes, one by one."

### Order Is Flexible
Lead with whichever element is strongest for your content. For business content, usually proof first — because the first question is always "why should I listen to you?"

For entertainment, you "confirm the thumbnail" (show the crazy thing). For education, you "edify the person" (prove expertise). Same principle, different execution.

## Key Takeaway
Every piece of educational content needs three things at the start: proof, promise, plan. The order is flexible, but all three must be present.

## When to Apply
- Videos have high click-through rate but low watch time
- Viewers drop off in the first 30 seconds
- Content is perceived as "not credible"

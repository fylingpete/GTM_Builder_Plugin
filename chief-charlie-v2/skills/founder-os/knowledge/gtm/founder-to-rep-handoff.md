# Prove non-founders can sell

Phase 2.2 is the **founder hand-off test**. Phase 2.1 produced a
playbook. Phase 2.2 answers: can someone who is NOT the founder
execute it and produce results?

The test is binary and brutal. You hire two ordinary sales reps. You
give them the playbook. You measure productivity per rep (PPR) against
their on-target earnings (OTE). At least one of them must hit >4× their
OTE in productivity, with customers still happy, before you can declare
2.2 done.

If they can't hit that bar — the playbook is wrong, the product is
wrong, or the segment is wrong. You do NOT fix it by hiring more reps.

## Why two reps, not one

Two reps in cohort is Skok's prescription, and it has three reasons:

- **Rep-A vs rep-B comparison.** If both struggle, the playbook is
  the problem. If one succeeds and one fails, the failing rep is the
  problem (or hiring is — see below).
- **Statistical floor.** One rep tells you nothing — they could be a
  unicorn or a dud.
- **Pod formation.** Skok and Kazanjy both push the "revenue
  mitochondria" model — SDR + AE + CSM as the cloneable unit. Two AEs
  is the start of the first pod.

Mark Gally's practitioner tactic: hire one **trailblazer** (similar
profile to the pathfinder from 2.1) and one **grinder** (executes the
playbook as written, doesn't deviate). The trailblazer tests playbook
edges; the grinder tests playbook stability.

## The 4× OTE rule

If a rep's OTE is $100K, they must generate >$400K in bookings (at the
target margin) within their ramp period. Anything less and the unit
economics will not survive scaling — Skok Phase 8 demands LTV:CAC > 3
and CAC-payback < 18 months, both of which depend on rep productivity.

**Ramp period** is typically 3-6 months for B2B SaaS, but the bar is
the *post-ramp* run rate, not month 1.

## The five mandatory artifacts

The playbook from 2.1 must now be expanded into **operational documents**
that a non-founder can use as a daily reference:

### 1. Sales playbook (full version)

The 2.1 playbook was the spine. The 2.2 version adds:

- ICP scoring rubric (how to qualify a lead in 60 seconds)
- Discovery-call script with branching paths
- Objection-handling map (top 10 objections with responses)
- Demo storyline (NOT a feature tour — see below)
- Pricing & negotiation guardrails
- Handoff-to-CS criteria

### 2. Demo / discovery script

YC's enterprise-funnel rule: **demo as a story, not a feature tour.**
The story is the buyer's, not the product's. Open with the buyer's
pain (you learned it in discovery), show the *one* part of the product
that solves it, end with the outcome. Three slides if possible. Feature
tours kill conversion.

### 3. Objection-handling guide

Hormozi's frameworks here, all writeable in one document:

- **Kill Zombies** — address obstacles BEFORE you mention price. It is
  much easier to handle concerns before the stakes go up.
- **CLOSER framework** — Clarify why they're here, Label the problem,
  Overview past attempts, Sell the vacation not the plane, Explain
  the solution, Reinforce the decision.
- **AAA Reframing** — never answer the question directly; ask
  questions about the question.
- **1-to-10 close** — ask the prospect to rate the deal 1-10, then
  walk them from their number to 10 to surface the real objection.
- **Never ask "do you have any questions?"** — it invites objections
  and hands the prospect the wheel.

### 4. Discovery-call coaching framework

Schatz's "black sheet" — a structured rubric for reviewing call
recordings. Every rep has weekly call reviews against the rubric for
the first 90 days. Without coaching, reps drift; the playbook
deteriorates within 8 weeks.

Hormozi's complementary rules:
- **Listen more than you talk** — best salespeople listen 2× as much
  as they talk. Prospects believe themselves, not you.
- **Breathe the script** — memorize until it's automatic. You can't
  listen if you're trying to remember what to say.
- **Pre-call prep** — 5 minutes of research builds more trust than
  30 minutes of rapport talk.

### 5. Onboarding plan for new reps

How long until a rep is closing solo? What artifacts do they read in
week 1, week 2, week 3? Who do they shadow? When do they take their
first call alone? Without this written down, every new rep onboards
differently and the cohort comparison breaks down.

## Founder's job during 2.2

The founder is NOT closing deals in 2.2. The founder's job is:

- **Coaching** — listen to call recordings, identify gaps, refine playbook
- **Playbook iteration** — the playbook will be wrong in places only
  visible when a rep tries to execute it. Fix it.
- **Hiring next reps** — if the first cohort works, who's next?
- **Stepping back from individual deals** — if the founder is on every
  call, the rep can't develop. Skok: founders who can't let go don't
  scale.

## Hiring signals (Schatz)

Schatz's interview rubric for ordinary reps in 2.2:

- Track record of **executing someone else's playbook** — not pathfinders
- **Ramp speed** at last role (months to first quota)
- **Coaching openness** — willingness to be reviewed weekly
- **Industry/buyer fit** — sold to the same persona before
- Red flag: heavy emphasis on "strategy", "branding", or "deals I
  brought in from my Rolodex"

For the structured interview itself, follow Hormozi's structured-interview
discipline: same questions, same scoring, every candidate.

## Exit criteria

Phase 2.2 is done when:

1. ≥1 of 2 reps is at >4× OTE productivity post-ramp
2. Customers from rep-led deals are as happy as customers from
   founder-led deals (CSAT or NPS parity)
3. The playbook documents are stable — no major changes in the last
   30 days
4. Founder has been off the closing seat for ≥30 days for those
   rep-led deals

The fourth criterion is what proves it's actually non-founder-saleable
versus "founder is whispering in the rep's ear on every call."

## Anti-patterns

- **Hiring 5 reps at once instead of 2.** If something's broken you
  burn 5× the cash before you find out.
- **Hiring before the 2.1 playbook is stable.** You hire reps to
  execute a playbook. No playbook = no execution.
- **Founder still on every call.** Skok: "If the founder is the
  closer, you don't have a non-founder-saleable motion. You have a
  founder doing the deal with someone else in the room."
- **Skipping the 4× OTE bar to keep a rep around.** A rep at 1.5×
  OTE will not make 2.5 (profitability) work. Cut early and rehire.
- **No coaching cadence.** Reps drift. Weekly reviews are the floor,
  not the ceiling.

## Connection to other steps

- **From 2.1:** The playbook is the input artifact. If it's incomplete,
  go back.
- **To 2.3 (Make growth scalable):** Once two reps work, you hire
  more reps AND build out demand-gen to feed them. The bookings-math
  formula (Skok: Bookings = #Reps × PPR) starts here.
- **To 2.4 (Customers succeed):** The handoff-to-CS criteria from the
  playbook is what feeds 2.4. If reps over-promise, churn spikes
  in 2.4.

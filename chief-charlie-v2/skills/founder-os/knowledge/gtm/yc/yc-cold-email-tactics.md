## Tactic: Seven Principles for Cold Emails That Convert — Y Combinator

Most cold emails are terrible — the bar is low to stand out. These seven principles put you in the top 5%.

### 1. One Focused Goal
Every word should drive toward a single action: reply, click, intro. Multiple asks create a paradox of choice — people delete or archive.

### 2. Be Human
Use emotions: "I'd love to," "it would mean a lot." Be informal — "Hey Aaron" beats "Hello Mr. Epstein." Typos are fine. Read your email out loud — if it sounds awkward, rewrite it. One person sent an animated GIF of herself waving with the recipient's name on a whiteboard. It worked.

### 3. Personalize with Uncommon Commonalities
"I see you're a man, I'm also a man" — useless. "We both had classes in Van Munching Hall" — instant connection. This requires research: use LinkedIn, their blog, their products. The deeper you go, the more responses you get.

### 4. Keep It Short
If they can't reply from their phone in seconds, they won't. Wall of text = instant delete.

### 5. Establish Credibility
YC badge, former employer, school, impressive customers as social proof, industry data. Shared connections create baseline trust.

### 6. Make It About the Reader
Reframe every "I" as "you." Tell your story as the quest to solve their problem. Use the language your users use to describe you — put that phrase on your website header too.

### 7. Clear Call to Action
End with one concrete next step as its own paragraph: "Reply to let me know," "Can you intro me to X?" Make it scannable.

### Follow-Up
Follow up 2-4 times with 2-3 day gaps. Get creative — one founder sent "Free donuts" as a subject line, then delivered actual donuts. If no response, don't get angry. Check back in a few months.

## Key Takeaway
Write each email as if composing to one specific person — personalize, be human, and make it dead simple to respond.

## When to Apply
- Your cold emails are getting ignored or deleted
- You're using templates with placeholder variables instead of real personalization
- You want to improve response rates without increasing volume

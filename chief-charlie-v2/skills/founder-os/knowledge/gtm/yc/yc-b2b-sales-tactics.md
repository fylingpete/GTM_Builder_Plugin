## Tactic: Moving B2B Deals Forward — Y Combinator

Once you have a sales process running, these concrete moves accelerate deal velocity.

### Find Your Internal Champion
Identify the person who will sell for you when you're not in the room. Treat them like a co-founder inside the company. Ask them: "Describe the last time you bought software like this — who had to approve it, what was the process?" Map the organization: economic buyer, technical approver, security gatekeeper, legal team, day-to-day users. Devise a plan to win over each stakeholder.

### Create Urgency
Set a defined closing date with your champion. They'll miss it almost every time, but it creates internal urgency. Use scarcity: "We can only take two enterprise customers this quarter."

### Get on a Plane
Physical visits work wonders. Email your champion: "I'll be in Houston next week — lunch?" Then book your flight after they confirm.

### Shortcut Legal
Don't get dragged into endless contract redlining. Ask yourself: "Is this clause company-ending or just annoying?" Accept anything that doesn't expose you to unlimited liability or IP transfer. Take less money if it avoids a full procurement process — a $10-20K credit card charge your champion can approve personally beats months of CFO negotiations.

### Post-Sale: Customer Success
Dedicate as much effort to onboarding as to closing. One company signed $4M in contracts but only implemented $2M — they lacked a customer success function. Get SOC 2 started early to remove security objections.

## Key Takeaway
Never leave a meeting without the next touchpoint scheduled — drive the buying process yourself instead of waiting for the customer to move.

## When to Apply
- Deals are stalling after initial enthusiasm
- Legal review is dragging contracts out for months
- You've signed contracts but customers aren't actually using the product

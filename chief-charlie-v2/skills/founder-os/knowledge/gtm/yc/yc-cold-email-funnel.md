## Framework: The Cold Email Conversion Funnel — Y Combinator

The best cold email hack is a warm intro — 2-3x better conversion than cold. Turn over every rock: friends, LinkedIn, alumni networks, employer alumni. But when warm intros aren't possible, a systematic cold email funnel works.

### Map Your Funnel Backwards
Start with your goal (1 customer) and work backwards with realistic conversion rates:
- **800 emails sent** → 400 opens (50%) → 40 responses (10%) → 10 demos (25%) → **1 customer** (10%)

This means sending dozens of emails per day, not a handful per week. If you expect a few emails to produce results, you'll conclude "sales doesn't work" — wrong, you just sent too few.

### The #1 Lever: Better Targeting
Better targeting beats better copy every time. It's higher leverage to send 100 targeted emails than 1,000 untargeted ones. One company selling shipment services emailed a digital downloads marketplace — no amount of perfect copy would convert that.

### Track and Optimize
Measure conversion rates at every step. Find the weakest link and focus there. Important: conversion rates decrease as you scale — if they're bad when you're doing it manually, they'll get worse with automation.

### Volume Discipline
- Block off hours per day specifically for outreach
- Start manually and personalized — don't automate before you've learned what works
- Founders should send these personally — recipients take CEO emails far more seriously
- The goal of each email is the next step in the funnel, not closing the sale

## Key Takeaway
Cold email is a numbers game with a learnable funnel — map it backwards, track every step, and fix the weakest conversion point before trying to scale.

## When to Apply
- You're sending a few emails and wondering why nobody responds
- You don't know your conversion rates at each funnel step
- You're considering automating outreach before doing it manually first

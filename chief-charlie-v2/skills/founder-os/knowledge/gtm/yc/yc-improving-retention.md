## Tactic: Five Ways to Improve Cohort Retention — Y Combinator

When your cohort curves aren't flattening, these five approaches — ordered by impact and ease — help fix them.

### 1. Acquire Better Users
Often the product is fine but you're targeting the wrong people. Google Photos saw terrible cohorts from a Gen Z marketing push — young people don't reminisce about life memories. The fix wasn't changing the product, it was targeting people who actually needed photo management. Slice your cohorts by dimension (country, device, acquisition channel, company size) to find which segments retain well and which don't.

### 2. Improve Onboarding and Activation
The cheapest and easiest improvement. Don't just build the tool — think about how you teach people to use it. What were they doing yesterday? What should change today? Get users to their "aha moment" as fast as possible.

### 3. Improve the Product
New use cases, reduced latency, simpler flows. You'll see it in the data — older cohorts perform worst, middle cohorts improve slightly, newest cohorts flatten sooner and higher.

### 4. Build Network Effects
If every new user makes the product better for existing users, cohort retention improves automatically as the network densifies. Focus on building good, dense networks around your current users rather than spreading thin.

### 5. The Holy Grail: Curves That Go Up
The best products don't just retain users — usage increases over time. Combine better product, better targeting, and network effects to achieve cohort curves that flatten and then rise. This is the layer cake chart: total users growing month over month, composed of thick layers from old cohorts that never leave.

### Diagnostic: Refresh Weekly
Check your curves weekly. When things go south, you want to know fast. But the qualitative insight — what to change — comes from talking to users, not staring at charts.

## Key Takeaway
Don't default to "improve the product" — first check if you're acquiring the wrong users or losing them at onboarding, which are faster and cheaper to fix.

## When to Apply
- Your cohort retention curves are declining toward zero
- You're growing users but total active users aren't accumulating
- You've improved the product repeatedly but retention isn't changing

## Mindset Shift: Founders Must Sell Their Own Product — Y Combinator

Sales before product-market fit is not a delegatable function. It requires vision, credibility, experimentation, and a tight feedback loop with the product team. This is founder work.

### Why You Can't Hire Your Way Out
Hiring a salesperson before you can sell yourself is like hiring a chef before you know what dish you're making. Pre-PMF sales is fundamentally entrepreneurial — it requires the founder's vision and the ability to pivot on the spot. If you can't sell your product, no one else can either.

### Technical Founders Have an Advantage
Sales isn't psychological tricks — it's helping people solve problems. Engineers are great at this. Two things make founders effective sellers:
1. **Expertise**: You're an expert on both the problem and the product
2. **Conviction**: You sincerely believe your product solves the problem

Customers sense authenticity. A founder who built the product and deeply understands the problem is more credible than any hired sales rep.

### The Biggest Anti-Pattern
Talking to anyone who will take your call instead of people who will actually buy. Easy conversations with startups and individual contributors feel like progress but produce useless feedback. You need the person with the problem, the budget, AND the decision-making authority.

### Sales Is Fundamentally About Listening
The biggest founder sales mistake is diving into the pitch. Great salespeople spend most of their time asking questions: What made you take this call? How bad is this problem? Who else does it affect? What's your budget? How does your organization buy software? Listening reveals whether this is a real customer or a time-waster.

## Key Takeaway
Sales is a learnable superpower — it helps not just with customers, but with fundraising and hiring too. Just get started and learn by doing.

## When to Apply
- You're a technical founder who thinks you need a "business person" to do sales
- You're considering hiring salespeople before you've closed deals yourself
- You're spending time on easy conversations instead of qualified prospects

## Framework: B2B Sales Progression Stages — Y Combinator

Most B2B founders get stuck in long, poorly defined design partnerships that produce no revenue. The goal is to progress through four stages as fast as possible.

### Stage 1: Design Partnership
Useful for learning a domain (law, accounting) but dangerous when poorly scoped. Most drag on for months with low customer engagement. Instead: identify one narrow burning problem, build a wedge product in 48 hours, test it. If it works, sell that wedge to 10 similar customers — don't overbuild a broad platform.

### Stage 2: Free Trial / Pilot
Define clear success metrics upfront. Use a value equation: if your customer service AI solves 20% of queries and saves $1M in salaries, you charge $200K — prove that math. Techniques: backtesting on historical data, side-by-side trials, limited geography rollouts. Always have the willingness-to-pay conversation.

### Stage 3: Paid Trial
Shorter, higher commitment. Get financial commitment upfront — even a small credit card charge shortcuts long procurement. Insist on dedicated resources, schedule check-ins every few days, keep the timeframe to 7-14 days. Track time-to-first-value as your North Star. Book the post-pilot review before the pilot starts.

### Stage 4: Recurring Revenue Contract with Opt-Out
The pro move. Monthly or annual contract with a 30-60 day opt-out period. If the customer does nothing, it auto-converts to a full contract. One sales process, recurring revenue.

## Key Takeaway
Stop crawling in endless design partnerships — progress through the stages to contractually recurring revenue as fast as your product maturity allows.

## When to Apply
- You've been in a design partnership for months with no revenue
- Customers keep asking for "just one more feature" before paying
- You're closing deals but each one requires a separate negotiation

## Tactic: Charge From Day One — Y Combinator

It's tempting to offer your product for free — free trials, unpaid pilots, "let's start with a demo period." But if you don't charge, you don't have customers, and you don't have a company. Payment is the strongest signal that you're providing real value.

### Why Free Trials Are Wrong for B2B

Consumer free trials work because they capture a credit card upfront and people forget to cancel. That model doesn't apply to B2B. For business customers, use these alternatives instead:

- **Money-back guarantee** — Charge upfront. If they're not happy in 30 or 60 days, refund them. This is far better than a free trial because they've committed real money
- **Opt-out from annual** — Let them pay month-to-month instead of the annual rate. They still pay, just with less lock-in

### How to Find the Right Price

**Increase your price until customers complain but still pay.** That's the right price. If nobody complains, you're too cheap. If they refuse to pay at all, that's a qualification signal — move on to the next customer.

### The Qualification Filter

If a prospect doesn't want to pay during the first call or meeting, that's a great signal that they're not a good customer for you. Don't try to convince them. Fire that lead and move on. Having a price creates a natural filter that separates real customers from tire-kickers.

### Resist the Fear of "No"

Most founders avoid charging because they're afraid of getting rejected on price. But a "no" because of price is infinitely more useful than a "yes" to a free pilot — it tells you either your price is wrong or they don't have the problem you think they do.

## Key Takeaway
Charge from the first customer. Money-back guarantee beats free trial. Raise prices until customers push back but still pay — that's your market rate.

## When to Apply
- You're offering free pilots hoping they'll convert to paid later
- You don't know how to price your product
- Prospects say yes to everything but never actually pay

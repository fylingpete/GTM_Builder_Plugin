## Framework: B2B Startup Metrics — Y Combinator

Track 4-5 key metrics before you launch — flying without instruments is flying blind.

### The Three Must-Have Metrics
1. **Revenue** — always the primary metric for B2B. Not GMV, not page views, not gross transaction value. One neobank showed 50% biweekly GTV growth but flat revenue — they were subsidizing transactions with cashback. Revenue doesn't lie.
2. **Burn rate** — monthly costs minus revenue. How fast your bank balance drops.
3. **Runway** — bank balance divided by burn rate. If not at the top of every investor update, investors assume you're hiding something.

### Net Dollar Retention (NDR)
The engine behind exponential B2B growth. Take your January cohort ($100K MRR from 10 customers). A year later: 2 customers churned (-$20K), 3 upsold to $20K each (+$30K). Net: $110K = 110% NDR.

- **Above 100%**: Cohorts grow over time — existing customers fund growth even if you sign nobody new
- **Below 100%**: Leaky bucket — scrambling to replace churning customers
- **Early-stage target**: 125-150%+ (you're underpriced, adding features, getting better at upselling)
- **Mature target**: 110-120%
- **Below 100%**: Something is wrong — invest in fixing churn before adding more sales

### Gross Margin
Revenue minus cost of goods sold per customer. Pure SaaS used to be 95% margin. AI companies paying OpenAI/Anthropic credits have real costs — treat free credits as cash. Aim for 80-90%. Never scale negative gross margins — fix them first.

### Retention Layer Cake
Stack monthly cohorts: if retention flattens, cohorts accumulate into a growing layer cake of revenue. If retention goes to zero, you're filling a leaky bucket — reaching a natural plateau no matter how hard you work.

### Metric Hygiene
- Agree on definitions company-wide and stick with them
- Don't change definitions when numbers look bad — you're only fooling yourself
- Don't over-dashboard (500 metrics is as bad as zero)
- Still talk to customers — metrics tell you IF something is wrong, customers tell you WHAT

## Key Takeaway
Revenue, burn rate, and runway at the top of every investor update — then go deep on net dollar retention and gross margin to understand whether your growth is healthy or hollow.

## When to Apply
- You're launching and haven't set up metrics tracking yet
- Your growth looks impressive but you're not sure if customers are sticking around
- You're scaling fast but unit economics feel off
